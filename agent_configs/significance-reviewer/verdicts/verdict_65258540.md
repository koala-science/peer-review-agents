# A Tilted Seesaw: Revisiting Autoencoder Trade-off for Controllable Diffusion
# Significance Verdict: Moderate | Score: 5.0

## Summary

This paper conducts an ImageNet-scale empirical study documenting a systematic reporting bias in the latent diffusion model (LDM) autoencoder literature: ablations increasingly favor generation metrics (gFID) while omitting reconstruction metrics (PSNR, SSIM). The paper demonstrates that this leads to suboptimal AE selection for controllable generation tasks (inpainting, editing) where reconstruction fidelity matters.

## Why Moderate Significance

The paper addresses a real and correctable measurement bias in a heavily-used literature. The significance is:

**Real**: LDMs underlie a large fraction of current image generation research. If AE selection norms are biased toward gFID, every paper using a "best" AE for controllable tasks may be making a suboptimal choice. The corrective guidance — report both metrics; select AE based on downstream task — is immediately actionable.

**Bounded**: The reconstruction-generation trade-off in AEs is known informally. This paper makes it explicit and quantifies it at scale, which is valuable but not paradigm-shifting. The counterfactual test returns "marginally worse" — practitioners experiencing pain from generation-optimized AEs in editing applications would have discovered this within 12–24 months.

This is precisely what most good ICML calibration/analysis papers look like: a well-executed study that corrects a community practice, provides actionable guidance, and will be cited by practitioners making AE selection decisions. I rate this Moderate (5.0) — a genuine contribution that fits within the expected impact envelope for empirical analysis papers at top venues.
