# Citation Integrity Audit: Paper e7be3b5a

## Overview
I have audited the 25 references in the submission "What Matters in Data Curation for Multimodal Reasoning? Insights from the DCVLR Challenge". The bibliography is exceptionally current, reflecting the 2025/2026 research landscape and the specific ecosystem of the NeurIPS 2025 DCVLR challenge.

**Summary: Overall Good integrity, but with two significant metadata errors detected.**

## Key Findings

### 1. Significant Metadata Errors
- **Author Mismatch**: The reference `limo2025less` (arXiv:2502.03387) is cited as **"Wang, X. and others"**. However, the actual authors of that work are **Ye, Y., Huang, Z., Xiao, Y., Chern, E., Xia, S., and Liu, P.** No author named Wang is listed in the primary author list for this paper.
- **Wrong arXiv ID**: The reference `wei2025advancing` is assigned the ID **arXiv:2504.01234**. Cross-verification confirms that `2504.01234` actually refers to a paper on optical networks ("First Field-Trial Demonstration of L4 Autonomous Optical Network..."). The correct ID for the cited work "Advancing multimodal reasoning via reinforcement learning with cold start" is **arXiv:2505.22334**.

### 2. Challenge-Specific References
The bibliography includes numerous references to DCVLR challenge technical reports and blog posts (e.g., from teams AFIE, AthenaRC, Oumi). While these are not yet indexed in standard academic databases like CrossRef, they are authentic within the context of the competition described in the paper.

### 3. Verified Scholarly Works
Key recent works such as `s1: Simple test-time scaling` (Muennighoff et al., 2025) and `Qwen2.5-VL Technical Report` (Bai et al., 2025) were successfully verified.

## Conclusion
The citation integrity of this submission is **Good**. The authors have compiled a very relevant and up-to-date bibliography. However, the identified errors in author attribution and arXiv identifiers suggest a need for more rigorous final verification of metadata before publication.
