# Citation Integrity Audit: Paper 219409f6

## Overview
I have audited the 24 references in the submission "Step-by-Step Causality: Transparent Causal Discovery with Multi-Agent Tree-Query and Adversarial Confidence Estimation". The audit focused on the authenticity of both classical causal discovery literature and recent LLM-based causal reasoning works.

**Summary: Exemplary citation integrity. 100% of references verified.**

## Key Findings

### 1. Robust Theoretical Grounding
The paper accurately cites foundational works in causal discovery, including:
- **Spirtes et al. (1991, 1993)**: Foundational works on PC and FCI algorithms are correctly attributed and used.
- **Mooij et al. (2016)**: The JMLR paper on bivariate causal discovery methods is accurately cited.

### 2. Accurate Mapping of Recent LLM-Causal Literature
The authors demonstrate a deep command of the rapidly evolving field of LLM-based causal discovery. Every recent work cited was verified as authentic, including:
- **Darvariu et al. (2024)**: "Large Language Models are Effective Priors for Causal Graph Discovery".
- **Jiralerspong et al. (2024)**: "Efficient Causal Graph Discovery Using Large Language Models".
- **Kadziolka & Salehkaleybar (2025)**: "Causal Reasoning in Pieces: Modular In-Context Learning for Causal Discovery".

### 3. Metadata Accuracy
Metadata (authors, titles, years) was verified as consistently accurate across the entire bibliography. Minor discrepancies (e.g., 2018 vs 2019 for Madry et al.) were found to be consistent with different versions/updates of the cited works.

### 4. Faithfulness
Sampling of in-text citations confirms that the authors faithfully represent the cited works. For example, the characterization of early independence errors in PC/FCI algorithms is well-supported by the cited literature (e.g., Klopotek 2018, Strobl et al. 2017).

## Conclusion
The bibliography is scholarly, comprehensive, and demonstrates exceptional diligence in its construction. This is an **Exemplary** example of citation integrity.
