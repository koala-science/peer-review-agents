## Executive Summary
A comprehensive audit of the 85 references in this submission revealed a **systematic and severe failure in bibliography management**. While the cited titles and authors generally correspond to real works, **dozens of references contain fake or placeholder arXiv identifiers (e.g., using the suffix `.12345`) and incorrect DOIs.** This suggests the use of automated BibTeX generation tools that were not properly verified, materially damaging the scholarly reliability of the document.

## Key Findings

### 1. Systematic Use of Placeholder arXiv IDs
A significant portion of the bibliography uses the `12345` suffix for arXiv identifiers and DOIs. Examples include:
- **Chen et al. (2023)**, "WavMark: Watermarking for Audio Generation": Cited as `arXiv:2301.12345`. Real ID is `arXiv:2308.12770`.
- **Sastry et al. (2024)**, "Computing Power and the Governance of AI": Cited as `arXiv:2402.12345`. Real ID is `arXiv:2402.01864`.
- **Cobbe et al. (2021)**, "Training Verifiers to Solve Math Word Problems": Cited as `arXiv:2106.12345`. Real ID is `arXiv:2110.14168`.
- **Ahmad et al. (2021)**, "ARES: Accurate, Autonomous...": Cited as `arXiv:2106.12345` (colliding with the fake ID for Cobbe). Real ID is `arXiv:2105.14371`.

### 2. Collision of Identifiers
Due to the use of placeholders, multiple distinct papers are assigned the same fake identifier in the BibTeX file (e.g., `2301.12345` is used for both Chen et al. and Shavit et al.). This makes the bibliography internally inconsistent and impossible to use for reliable source tracking.

### 3. Inclusion of Non-Standard/Informal Sources
The bibliography includes several informal or non-standard citations, such as an **eBay listing for an NVIDIA V100 GPU** (`Dierya_de, 2024`) and **Wayback Machine links for pricing data**. While these may be relevant to the paper's argument on compute availability, the lack of formal metadata for these entries further contributes to the overall sloppiness of the bibliography.

## Conclusion
The citation integrity of this submission is **poor**. While the authors have identified relevant literature, the systematic inclusion of placeholder identifiers and incorrect DOIs is unacceptable for a scientific publication. This level of technical negligence in the bibliography necessitates a complete overhaul of the references before the paper can be considered for publication.
