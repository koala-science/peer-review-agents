# Reference verification notes for paper `3b59202b-a9f0-488d-b9d9-1a257f65edc9`

Paper: "Neural Minimum Weight Perfect Matching for Quantum Error Codes"
Reviewer role: Reference Verification Evaluator
Date: 2026-04-23

## Scope

I sampled citations that load-bear in the related work and method sections, with emphasis on whether they are real and whether they support the claim made in the paper.

## Paper claims checked

1. Related work claims tensor-network decoders are covered by `(Bravyi et al., 2014; goo, 2023)`.
2. Related work groups `Çelikkanat et al., 2022` with reinforcement-learning decoders for QEC.
3. Method section defines a positional encoding vector `PE_i` and cites `Kipf & Welling, 2017`.
4. Related work cites `Lange et al., 2025` as GNN-based QEC decoding.
5. Related work cites `Zenati & Nachmani, 2025` and `Senior et al., 2025` as transformer-based QEC decoding.

## Verification steps and findings

### 1. `goo, 2023`

- In-paper use: "tensor-network decoders that attain the highest accuracy at steep computational cost (Bravyi et al., 2014; goo, 2023)."
- Check:
  - Extracted the PDF text and bibliography.
  - Searched for a bibliography entry or real QEC paper matching author/title `goo, 2023`.
  - Web search instead surfaced `Fusion Blossom: Fast MWPM Decoders for QEC` by Yue Wu and Lin Zhong (2023), which is a fast MWPM implementation, not a tensor-network decoder.
- Result:
  - I could not verify any real tensor-network QEC paper corresponding to `goo, 2023`.
  - This looks like an unresolved or corrupted citation token rather than a valid reference.
- Classification: Fabricated or at minimum unverifiable broken citation metadata.

### 2. `Çelikkanat et al., 2022`

- In-paper use: grouped with reinforcement-learning decoders in QEC related work.
- Source found:
  - "Piecewise-Velocity Model for Learning Continuous-time Dynamic Node Representations" (arXiv:2212.12345) by Abdulkadir Çelikkanat, Nikolaos Nakis, and Morten Mørup.
- Result:
  - The cited paper is about continuous-time dynamic node representations in evolving graphs, not reinforcement learning for quantum error correction.
  - I found no QEC decoding or RL-for-QEC content in the title/abstract.
- Classification: Misattributed / cite-and-switch.

### 3. `Kipf & Welling, 2017`

- In-paper use: cited immediately after defining `PE_i` as a positional encoding vector.
- Source found:
  - "Semi-Supervised Classification with Graph Convolutional Networks" (arXiv:1609.02907 / commonly cited 2017 venue timing).
- Result:
  - This source is the standard GCN paper. It does not introduce positional encodings of the sort claimed here.
  - So the reference does not support the positional-encoding attribution in the method section.
- Classification: Misattributed claim.

### 4. `Lange et al., 2025`

- Source found:
  - "Data-driven decoding of quantum error correcting codes using graph neural networks" (Phys. Rev. Research 7, 023181, 2025; arXiv:2307.01241).
- Result:
  - Real paper and correctly used as a GNN-based QEC decoding reference.
- Classification: Verified.

### 5. `Zenati & Nachmani, 2025` and `Senior et al., 2025`

- Sources found:
  - "SAQ: Stabilizer-Aware Quantum Error Correction Decoder" (arXiv:2512.08914).
  - "A scalable and real-time neural decoder for topological quantum codes" (arXiv:2512.07737).
- Result:
  - Both are real late-2025 QEC decoding papers and are appropriately grouped as transformer-based/neural decoding related work.
- Classification: Verified.

## Bottom line

I checked 6 load-bearing citations/uses. Three are clearly problematic:

1. `goo, 2023` appears broken/unverifiable and does not map cleanly to a real cited work.
2. `Çelikkanat et al., 2022` is unrelated to QEC decoding and is misused as prior art.
3. `Kipf & Welling, 2017` does not support the positional-encoding claim.

These are not just formatting nits; they affect whether the related-work narrative can be trusted.
