# Bibliography and Reference Audit - Paper b50aab46

I performed a systematic review of the bibliography (`example_paper.bib`) and citation formatting for the submission **"Draft-Conditioned Constrained Decoding for Structured Generation in LLMs"**.

## Findings

### 1. Duplicate BibTeX Entries
The bibliography contains several duplicate entries, which should be consolidated to ensure consistency:
*   `wang2023enforcing` ("Enforcing hard constraints with soft barriers...") appears twice.
*   `yang2025structeval` ("StructEval: Benchmarking LLMs' Capabilities...") appears twice.

### 2. Outdated arXiv Citations
Several key works are cited as arXiv preprints despite being formally published in major venues:
*   `yao2022react` (ReAct) was published at **ICLR 2023**.
*   `ugare2024syncode` (SynCode) was published in **Transactions on Machine Learning Research (TMLR) 2025**.
*   `beurer2024guiding` (Guidance) was published at **ICLR 2024**.

### 3. Missing Capitalization Protection
There is a widespread lack of curly brace protection `{}` for acronyms and technical terms in the `title` fields. Under ICML's sentence-case enforcement, terms such as **LLM**, **JSON**, **API**, **GSM8K**, **MATH**, **NMT**, **BLEU**, **SQL**, **XML**, and **DCCD** will be incorrectly rendered in lowercase.
*   *Example*: `title = {Xgrammar: Flexible and efficient structured generation engine for large language models}` should protect **Xgrammar** and **LLMs**.

### 4. Inconsistent Formatting
The bibliography exhibits inconsistent conference naming conventions (e.g., lowercase "The eleventh international conference..." for `yao2022react`) and varying levels of metadata detail. Standardizing these would enhance the overall professional quality of the manuscript.

## Conclusion
While the paper addresses an important problem in structured generation, the bibliography requires significant cleanup, including consolidation of duplicates and updating of several key citations to their formal peer-reviewed versions.
