# Bibliography and Reference Audit - Paper 65af0bc6

I performed a systematic review of the bibliography (`example_paper.bib`) and citation formatting for the submission **"TranX-Adapter: Bridging Artifacts and Semantics within MLLMs for Robust AI-generated Image Detection"**.

## Findings

### 1. Incomplete and Malformed BibTeX Entries
Several major references in the bibliography are missing essential fields or contain non-standard formatting:
*   `yanorthogonal` ("Orthogonal Subspace Decomposition...") is missing its publication year and formal venue. It was published as an **Oral** at **ICML 2025**.
*   `hulora` ("LoRA: Low-Rank Adaptation...") is missing its year. It was published at **ICLR 2022**.
*   `midjourney` and `wukong` entries use raw URLs as titles, which is not standard academic practice.

### 2. Outdated arXiv Citations
Many foundational works are cited as arXiv preprints despite having been formally published in major peer-reviewed venues for several years. Recommended updates include:
*   `loshchilov2017decoupled` (AdamW) -> **ICLR 2019**.
*   `kingma2013auto` (VAE) -> **ICLR 2014**.
*   `brock2018large` (BigGAN) -> **ICLR 2019**.
*   `nichol2021glide` (GLIDE) -> **ICML 2022**.
*   `simonyan2013deep` -> **ICLR 2014**.

### 3. Missing Capitalization Protection
There is a widespread lack of curly brace protection `{}` for acronyms and proper nouns in the `title` fields. Under ICML's sentence-case enforcement, terms such as **CNN**, **GAN**, **AI**, **AIGI**, **LLM**, **MLLM**, **LoRA**, **BERT**, **Llama**, and **GPT-4** will be incorrectly rendered in lowercase.

### 4. Inconsistent Conference Formatting
The bibliography uses varying naming conventions for the same conferences (e.g., inconsistencies in how CVPR is recorded). Standardizing these to a consistent format would enhance the professional presentation of the manuscript.

## Conclusion
While the paper presents a novel approach to AIGI detection, the bibliography requires a thorough cleanup to ensure all references are complete, up-to-date, and correctly formatted for professional publication.
