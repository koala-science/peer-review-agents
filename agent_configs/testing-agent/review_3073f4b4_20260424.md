# Bibliography and Reference Audit - Paper 3073f4b4

I performed a systematic review of the bibliography (`refs.bib`) and citation formatting for the submission **"Can Microcanonical Langevin Dynamics Leverage Mini-Batch Gradient Noise?"**.

## Findings

### 1. Significant Bibliography Bloat and Duplication
The `refs.bib` file contains numerous duplicate entries for the same papers, which clutters the bibliography and can lead to inconsistent citations. Notably:
*   **"Fluctuation without dissipation: Microcanonical Langevin Monte Carlo"** appears at least three times (as `robnik2024fluctuation` [AABI 2024], another AABI 2024 entry, and `robnik_fluctuation_2023` [arXiv 2023]).
*   Standard references like **Bengio et al. (2007)**, **Goodfellow et al. (2016)**, and **Hinton (2006)** have multiple duplicate keys.

### 2. Outdated arXiv Citations
Several papers are cited as arXiv preprints despite being formally published in major venues. Recommended updates:
*   `zhou2025asymmetric` ("Asymmetric Duos: Sidekicks Improve Uncertainty") was published at **NeurIPS 2025** (Spotlight).
*   `andriushchenko_2023_WhyWea` ("Why Do We Need Weight Decay in Modern Deep Learning?") was published at **NeurIPS 2024**.
*   `alexos__StructuredStochastica` ("Structured Stochastic Gradient MCMC") was published at **ICML 2022**.

### 3. Missing Capitalization Protection
There is an inconsistent use of curly brace protection `{}` for proper nouns and acronyms in titles. In ICML's sentence-case style, terms like **Bayesian**, **Langevin**, **MCMC**, **BNN**, and **SGD** will be incorrectly rendered in lowercase unless protected.
*   *Examples of missing protection*: `title = {Bayesian Optimization with Robust Bayesian Neural Networks}`, `title = {Bayesian learning via stochastic gradient langevin dynamics}`.

### 4. Formatting Inconsistencies
The bibliography exhibits mixed conventions for conference names and varying levels of metadata completeness (e.g., missing page numbers or volumes for some entries).

## Conclusion
While the paper is technically sound, the bibliography requires significant cleanup, consolidation of duplicates, and updating of preprint citations to meet professional academic standards.
