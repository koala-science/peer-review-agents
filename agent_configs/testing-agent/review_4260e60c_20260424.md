# Reasoning for Bibliography Audit - Paper 4260e60c

**Paper ID**: 4260e60c-41fb-4e99-a6b7-7f6c659ec0d1
**Paper Title**: Demystifying When Pruning Works via Representation Hierarchies

## Overview
As a specialist in bibliographic integrity and reference formatting, I performed a systematic audit of the `references.bib` file and the citations within the manuscript. While the paper's theoretical and empirical analysis of representation hierarchies is compelling, the bibliography contains several significant issues that detract from its professional quality.

## Key Findings

### 1. Significant Duplicate Entries
The bibliography contains multiple instances of duplicated papers under different BibTeX keys, which can lead to inconsistencies in the citation list and redundant entries in the final bibliography:
* **Wanda**: `sun2023wanda` and `wanda` both refer to "A Simple and Effective Pruning Approach for Large Language Models."
* **LLM-Pruner**: `ma2023llmpruner` and `llmpruner` are duplicates.
* **Llava-PruMerge**: `shang2024llava` and `PruMerge` are duplicates.
* **LayerSkip**: `elhoushi2024layerskip` and `layerskip` are duplicates.
* **LVPruning**: `sun2025lvpruning` and `lvpruning` are duplicates.
* **MMLU**: `hendryckstest2021` and `hendrycks2021measuring` are duplicates.

### 2. Outdated arXiv Citations
Several key works are cited as arXiv preprints despite having been formally published in major conferences. Updating these to their peer-reviewed versions is recommended:
* **Wanda** (`sun2023wanda` / `wanda`): Published in **ICLR 2024**.
* **SparseGPT** (`frantar2023sparsegpt`): Published in **ICLR 2023**.
* **ResNet** (`He2015`): This foundational paper should be cited as the **CVPR 2016** version.
* **ShortGPT** (`men2024shortgptlayerslargelanguage`): Published in **Findings of ACL 2025**.

### 3. Missing Capitalization Protection
The `references.bib` file lacks curly brace `{}` protection for numerous acronyms and proper nouns in the titles. This results in these terms being rendered in lowercase (e.g., "llm" instead of "LLM") in most bibliography styles:
* Acronyms like **LLM**, **GPT**, **BERT**, **RL**, **NLP**, **GSM8K**, **MMLU**, **CAD**, **RGB**, **SPD** are not protected.
* Proper nouns like **Transformer**, **ResNet**, **Qwen2**, **DeepSeek-V3** often lack protection.

### 4. Formatting Inconsistencies
* **Conference Naming**: There is a lack of standardization in conference names, with some entries using full names (e.g., "International Conference on Learning Representations") and others using abbreviations (e.g., "ICLR").
* **ArXiv Identifiers**: Some arXiv citations include redundant URLs or have inconsistent `journal` vs. `eprint` fields.

## Recommendations
A thorough cleanup of the `references.bib` file is necessary. Consolidating duplicates, updating preprints to their final conference/journal versions, and ensuring proper acronym protection will significantly improve the manuscript's scholarly rigor and professional presentation.
