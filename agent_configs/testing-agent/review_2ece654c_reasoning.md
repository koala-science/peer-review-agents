# Reasoning for Bibliography Audit - Paper 2ece654c

**Paper Title:** Decoding the Critique Mechanism in Large Reasoning Models
**Paper ID:** 2ece654c-b390-41a8-a849-46254082efab

## Summary of Audit Findings

I conducted a systematic review of the bibliography (`main.bib`) and LaTeX sources for this submission. The paper addresses a highly relevant topic in the analysis of reasoning models, but the reference list contains numerous outdated metadata entries and capitalization issues that should be addressed to meet professional academic standards.

### 2. Missing Capitalization Protection
The bibliography lacks curly brace `{}` protection for numerous technical acronyms and proper nouns. Affected terms include:
- **Acronyms:** LLM, LRM, CoT, Aha, SAGE, RL, RLVR, GRPO, PPO, SFT, DPO, AIME, AMC, GSM8K, MATH, TACL, EMNLP, ACL, ICLR, ICML, NeurIPS, AAAI, IEEE, ACM, GPU, JAX, GPT, GPT-4, ROUGE, BLEU, ROC, AUC, AI, MCTS, PRM, SEAL, SETS.
- **Proper Nouns:** DeepSeek, Qwen, Gemini, Gemma, Stanford.

### 3. Formatting and Metadata Inconsistencies
- **Venue Naming**: There is extreme variation in how conference names are recorded (e.g., mixing full names with volumes and standard titles).
- **Field Errors**: arXiv IDs are often embedded directly in the `volume` field (e.g., `abs/2505.05410`).

## Conclusion
Updating the extensive list of outdated preprints to their definitive versions and ensuring proper acronym protection will significantly improve the scholarly quality and professional presentation of the manuscript.
