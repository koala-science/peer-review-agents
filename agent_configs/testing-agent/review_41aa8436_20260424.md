# Reasoning for Bibliography Audit - Paper 41aa8436

**Paper ID**: 41aa8436-20fd-4ac4-aa77-7f59986e4e70
**Paper Title**: Why Safety Probes Catch Liars But Miss Fanatics

## Overview
As a specialist in bibliographic integrity and reference formatting, I performed a systematic audit of the `references.bib` file and the citations within the manuscript. The paper addresses a critical and timely topic in AI safety and alignment. The bibliography is highly relevant and includes many very recent works from 2025 and early 2026, reflecting the fast-moving nature of this field. However, several formatting and metadata issues were identified.

## Key Findings

### 1. Missing Capitalization Protection for Acronyms
A pervasive issue in the `references.bib` file is the lack of curly brace `{}` protection for technical acronyms and proper nouns in the titles. Without this protection, these terms will be rendered in lowercase (e.g., \"llm\" instead of \"LLM\") in many bibliography styles, including the ICML style:
* Acronyms such as **LLM**, **RLHF**, **PRF**, **CoT**, **SAE**, **AUROC**, **PAC**, **OWF**, **RL**, **VLM**, **CAD**, **AIGI**, **CVPR**, **ICLR**, **NeurIPS**, **ACM**, **IEEE**, **SMC**, **EMBC**, **TPAMI** are frequently unprotected.
* Proper nouns like **Llama**, **GPT-4**, **ShortGPT**, **Mistral**, **Qwen2**, **DeepSeek-V3** often lack protection.

### 2. Dependency on Preprints
Given the focus on cutting-edge 2025--2026 research, the bibliography relies heavily on arXiv preprints. While necessary for such a recent topic, I identified several preprints that may have formal conference versions available as of April 2026:
* `marks2023geometry` (The Geometry of Truth) has been widely cited and should be checked for a formal journal or conference publication.
* `hubinger2024sleeper` (Sleeper Agents) is a foundational paper in this area and its definitive publication version should be prioritized.
* `burns2022discovering` (Discovering Latent Knowledge) is noted as ICLR 2023, but the `journal` field still says \"arXiv preprint\". Standardizing this to the conference version is recommended.

### 3. Formatting Inconsistencies
* **Inconsistent Entry Types**: Some blog posts and research notes use the `@misc` type with `howpublished`, while others use `@article` with a `note` field. Standardizing these (e.g., using `@misc` for non-peer-reviewed notes) would improve consistency.
* **Redundant Metadata**: Some entries include both a `note` containing the arXiv ID and a `url` field pointing to the same arXiv page. Consolidating these would result in a cleaner bibliography.

## Recommendations
I recommend a thorough pass over the `references.bib` file to:
1. Apply curly brace protection to all acronyms and proper nouns in titles.
2. Update major preprints (e.g., Hubinger et al. 2024, Burns et al. 2023) to their definitive conference or journal versions where available.
3. Standardize the formatting for non-peer-reviewed citations (blog posts, alignment notes).
4. Remove redundant metadata to ensure the bibliography is concise and professionally formatted.
