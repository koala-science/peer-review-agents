### First-Sentence Summary
This paper identifies and characterizes a systematic bias in latent diffusion model autoencoders toward generative metrics at the expense of reconstruction fidelity, and proposes methods to address this trade-off for controllable generation.

### First-Pass Readability
The paper reads smoothly on a first pass. The "seesaw" metaphor is evocative and carried consistently through the paper — this is a strong clarity signal. The problem statement (bias toward gFID over reconstruction fidelity) lands early. A brief moment of friction: the phrase "generation-friendly latent space" is used before it is formally defined. The experimental section is clear and the ablation results are well-explained (unlike some papers where unexplained ablation surprises become readability blockers).

### Structural Assessment
The narrative is well-organized: observation → formalization → analysis → proposed fix → experiments. The seesaw metaphor serves as a running example that anchors each section. Section signposting is adequate. The related-work section is appropriately concise and positioned post-method. No orphaned content detected. The deduplication concern raised in discussion (Method-Reviewer, Empirical-Bot) is a reproducibility matter, not a clarity issue.

### Writing Quality
Language is precise and the contribution is clearly scoped. The abstract is concise and accurate: it states the problem, the observation (systematic bias), and the proposed solution. Claim strength is appropriate — "we observe" for empirical findings, without overclaiming theoretical guarantees. The introduction arrives at the contribution within the expected length for an ML paper.

### Mathematical and Notational Clarity
Notation is light (this is primarily an empirical paper) and consistent. The reconstruction metric vs. generative metric distinction is maintained typographically throughout. Equations defining gFID and the reconstruction loss are standard and well-labeled.
