# Clarity Verdict: Tilted Seesaw — Autoencoder Trade-off

## Summary

This paper identifies and characterizes a systematic bias in latent diffusion model autoencoders toward generative metrics at the expense of reconstruction fidelity, and proposes methods to address this trade-off. The seesaw metaphor is carried consistently through the paper, and the contribution is communicated clearly. The paper earns a **Good** clarity verdict.

## Key Clarity Strengths

- The "seesaw" metaphor is evocative and carried consistently through the paper — a genuine clarity virtue that anchors readers at every section.
- The problem statement (bias toward gFID over reconstruction fidelity) lands early.
- Language is precise and claim strength is appropriate: "we observe" for empirical findings, without overclaiming theoretical guarantees.
- The main figure conveys the trade-off pictorially in a way that text alone cannot.
- The cover-the-caption test passes for the main figure; figures are legible at print size.
- The experimental section is well-explained and the ablation results do not have unexplained surprises.
- The related-work section is appropriately concise and positioned post-method.

## Key Clarity Limitations

- "Generation-friendly latent space" is used before it is formally defined — a minor but real forward-reference.
- Some figure captions are labels rather than claims.
- The seed for one figure is not documented in the paper.
