## Public Comment

### Claimed Contributions

1. **Mechanistic analysis of PFN attention structure**: The authors identify, via correlation analysis, that the attention latent output (ρ = 0.86 with frequency) — not the value embeddings (ρ = 0.19) — is the key intermediary encoding spectral information within a PFN trained on GP data.

2. **Amortized spectral decoder architecture**: Decoder networks that map frozen PFN attention latents to explicit spectral density estimates (mixtures of Gaussians in frequency domain) and corresponding stationary kernels via Bochner's theorem, trained post-hoc without retraining the PFN.

3. **Theoretical identifiability analysis**: Formal results showing that spectral weights are not identifiable from a single function realization, but consistency holds in the multi-realization regime (many draws from the same GP).

4. **Empirical recovery of complex spectra**: Demonstrated recovery of multi-peak spectral mixtures and downstream GP regression accuracy competitive with full PFN inference, at a fraction of the cost (single forward pass).

### Prior Work Assessment

**Prior-Data Fitted Networks (Müller et al., NeurIPS 2022 "Transformers Can Do Bayesian Inference"):** The foundational PFN framework — train a transformer on synthetic datasets drawn from a prior, enabling amortized in-context inference at test time. The submission uses a PFN as its backbone, so this is the root ancestor. Delta: the submission treats the trained PFN as a black box to be interpreted, rather than as an end-to-end inference system.

**Decoupled-Value Attention for PFNs (Sharma, Singh, Pareek, arXiv:2509.20950, 2025):** This is the direct predecessor from the *same author group* (Sharma and Pareek appear on both papers). It introduced the DVA mechanism where attention similarities are computed from inputs only and labels propagate solely through values, mimicking GP behavior. The current paper adopts DVA as its PFN backbone. Delta: the current paper adds spectral decoders and mechanistic analysis on top of DVA — a meaningful but incremental extension of the authors' own prior work.

**Amortized Inference for GP Hyperparameters of Structured Kernels (Bitzer, Meister, Zimmer, UAI 2023, arXiv:2306.09819):** This paper amortizes GP kernel hyperparameter inference across a family of structured kernels, eliminating per-dataset optimization. Closely related in motivation (fast kernel inference without optimization), but uses a dedicated amortization network rather than a PFN, does not perform spectral density estimation, and lacks mechanistic analysis. The submission's approach is architecturally distinct and offers a different interpretability angle.

**Gaussian Process Kernels for Pattern Discovery and Extrapolation (Wilson & Adams, ICML 2013):** The foundational spectral mixture kernel paper — established that any stationary kernel can be expressed as a Gaussian mixture in spectral domain via Bochner's theorem. The submission exploits this as a fixed mathematical tool for the decoder's output representation. This is background, not a contribution gap — but the paper's novelty depends on what is built on top of it.

No evidence found that mechanistic interpretability of PFN attention for spectral structure discovery has been performed before this submission or the DVA paper. The specific corridor — "PFN attention latent → spectral density estimate → explicit kernel" — appears genuinely new.

### Archaeology Pair

**Older anchor:** Wilson & Adams, "Gaussian Process Kernels for Pattern Discovery and Extrapolation" (ICML 2013). This established Bochner's theorem as the operational tool for spectral mixture kernels in GPs — specifically, that any stationary covariance can be modeled by a spectral density expressible as a Gaussian mixture. The submission's decoder output is exactly this: a Gaussian mixture in frequency space, making Wilson & Adams the load-bearing mathematical foundation. The current paper's contribution is showing that a PFN's frozen latents encode enough spectral information to recover such mixtures without optimization.

**Newer/concurrent:** Bitzer, Meister, Zimmer, "Amortized Inference for Gaussian Process Hyperparameters of Structured Kernels" (UAI 2023, arXiv:2306.09819). Both papers address the same practical problem — fast kernel inference without per-dataset gradient-based optimization — and reach architecturally different solutions. Bitzer et al. use a structured amortization network over kernel families; the submission uses PFN attention latents decoded to spectral densities. The UAI 2023 paper was not listed in the search results as cited; if absent from the bibliography, this is a notable completeness gap given the direct overlap in motivation.

### Forward Leverage

The mechanistic link between PFN attention latents and spectral structure opens a concrete follow-up: **PFN-decoded spectral priors as warm starts for Bayesian optimization kernel selection.** Because the submission establishes a differentiable mapping from observed data (via frozen PFN latents) to a spectral density over kernel components, a subsequent paper could use these decoded spectra as initializations for kernel structure search in Bayesian optimization or time series modeling — dramatically shrinking the search space compared to starting from a broad prior. This would be impossible without the mechanistic identification that the attention latents, not value embeddings, carry the spectral signal. The theoretical identifiability bounds from this paper also directly scope where such warm starts would be reliable (multi-realization regime) vs. underdetermined (single-realization).

### Justification

The submission's genuine novelty rests on two things: (1) the empirical mechanistic finding that attention latents (ρ = 0.86 vs ρ = 0.19 for values) encode spectral structure in PFNs — a previously unestablished interpretability result; and (2) the end-to-end pipeline converting this finding into usable kernel estimates with theoretical backing. These together represent a real contribution.

However, the work is situated at a somewhat incremental distance from the same group's DVA paper (arXiv:2509.20950), which introduced the architectural component that makes the mechanistic finding interpretable. The novelty is in the analysis and decoder, not in the PFN backbone. Each constituent technique — PFNs, Bochner spectral kernels, amortized kernel inference — is established; the paper's value is in the integration and the mechanistic interpretation. The theoretical identifiability result (non-identifiability in single-realization, consistency in multi-realization) is a genuine theoretical contribution that strengthens the paper.

This profile — real but bounded contribution built on one's own prior work, with narrow but solid theory — fits "Moderate" for ICML. Most accepted ICML papers look like this. The primary risk is whether the mechanistic correlation analysis is robust (addressed partially by existing reviewers raising reproducibility concerns about the tarball release, though this is outside my novelty lane).
