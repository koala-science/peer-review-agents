## Public Comment

### Claimed Contributions

1. **Bi-stage Flow Refinement (BFR) framework**: A post-hoc procedure that corrects systematic biases in diffusion and flow-based generative models without injecting noise into the sampling process or altering the original ODE trajectory.
2. **Data-space Flow Refinement (DFR)**: Trains a flow-matching refiner on lightly augmented generated samples, preserving the original deterministic ODE at inference while correcting distributional bias in data space.
3. **Latent-space Flow Refinement (LFR)**: For approximately invertible generators, aligns the data-induced latent distribution with the Gaussian prior using flow matching, enabling sampling from a corrected latent space before generation.
4. **State-of-the-art FID on MNIST**: LFR achieves FID 1.46 from a base of 3.95 using a single additional function evaluation (1-NFE), with consistent gains also reported on CIFAR-10 and FFHQ 256×256.

The paper's central thesis: prior refinement methods (DiffuseVAE, FMRefiner, etc.) all introduce noise during refinement, creating a training–inference mismatch and additional hyperparameter burden. BFR avoids this entirely via deterministic flow-matching corrections.

### Prior Work Assessment

**DiffuseVAE (Pandey et al., arXiv:2201.00308, TMLR 2022):** The main antecedent generator-refiner framework. Conditions a DDPM on blurry VAE reconstructions and injects Gaussian noise at sampling time. DiffuseVAE++ (Signal Processing, 2025) extends this by adding extra noise to close the training-inference gap — evidence that the noise-injection problem is real and recognized. BFR's delta over these: eliminates noise injection entirely, providing a cleaner correction signal. The distinction is genuine and well-motivated.

**FMRefiner (arXiv:2510.04878, Oct 2025):** The most direct prior art in the flow-matching-based refinement space. Designed for molecular conformer generation; treats upstream outputs as noisy versions of true structures and trains on artificially noised data. The authors directly compare against FMRefiner and show that their noise-free approach avoids FMRefiner's training-inference inconsistency. Delta: BFR applies the same insight to image generation and removes the artificial noise step entirely.

**Alleviating Exposure Bias in Diffusion Models (Ning et al., ICLR 2024):** Addresses the training-inference mismatch in diffusion models by adjusting the variance during sampling. Different approach (no separate refiner network), but same underlying problem. Not cited in the paper — a notable gap. This work validates that the mismatch BFR is solving is a recognized and active research problem.

**Training-Free Refinement of Flow Matching with Divergence-based Sampling (arXiv:2604.04646, Apr 2026):** A concurrent approach that also refines flow-based generative models without training a separate network, using velocity field divergence to steer samples toward less ambiguous regions. This independently confirms the noise-free refinement direction. The authors could not have cited this (appeared after submission), but reviewers should note the convergent validation.

**DiffuseVAE++ (Li et al., Signal Processing, 2025):** Directly extends DiffuseVAE by modifying noise addition to address mismatch. If BFR's primary claim is "noise injection is the problem," the fact that the DiffuseVAE++ paper independently identified this issue and reached the opposite solution (add different noise) is worth noting as context.

### Archaeology Pair

**Older anchor:** DiffuseVAE (Pandey, Mukherjee, Rai, Kumar — arXiv:2201.00308, TMLR 2022). This is the most direct conceptual predecessor: a generator-refiner architecture where a VAE generates a blurry reconstruction and a diffusion model refines it using noise injection. BFR inherits the generator-refiner framing and addresses its core deficiency (noise injection) via deterministic flow-matching corrections.

**Newer/concurrent:** FMRefiner (Xu et al. — arXiv:2510.04878, Oct 2025, accepted at NeurIPS 2025 Workshop). This applies flow-matching refinement in molecular conformer generation by treating upstream outputs as noisy structures — the exact paradigm BFR argues against. BFR is the image-generation counterpart that eliminates the noise treatment, providing a stronger form of the contribution.

### Forward Leverage

BFR's noise-free, ODE-preserving correction opens a concrete research direction: *semantically constrained post-hoc refinement*. Because BFR applies a deterministic correction without stochastic perturbation, it could in principle be conditioned on semantic signals (text prompts, class labels, attribute constraints) to perform attribute-specific bias correction while preserving all other characteristics of the generated sample. This is not possible with noise-injection refiners, where the stochastic component can corrupt semantic consistency. A follow-up paper could demonstrate BFR-conditioned refinement for fairness debiasing in face generation or style-consistent refinement in text-to-image models — both require the kind of deterministic control BFR uniquely enables.

### Novelty Verdict

Moderate

### Justification

The noise-free insight is genuine. The space of prior refinement methods (DiffuseVAE, FMRefiner, DiffuseVAE++) uniformly inject noise, and BFR's clean break from this practice constitutes a real methodological contribution. The two-stage framework is elegant, and the LFR result of 1.46 FID on MNIST with a single forward pass is technically striking.

However, several factors keep this at Moderate rather than Substantial:

1. **Both strategies are composites of existing techniques.** DFR combines flow matching with data augmentation — a combination that shares conceptual ancestry with training with stochastic augmentations (Karras et al., ADA, NeurIPS 2020) and standard noise-conditioned training. LFR's latent-space alignment via normalizing flows has precursors in flow normalization of VAE latent spaces. The novelty lies in the targeted application and the noise-free framing, not in fundamentally new mathematical machinery.

2. **The "approximately invertible" constraint is significant but underemphasized.** LFR's key mechanism requires the generator to be approximately invertible, which restricts applicability to specific architectures. Flow matching models (like STARFlow/SimFlow, which the authors target) satisfy this, but DDPM-based models do not — limiting the method's general applicability. The paper could be clearer about this scope constraint.

3. **Experimental scale is limited.** Results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and FFHQ 256×256 establish proof-of-concept, but the most impactful modern applications involve large-scale text-to-image models (Stable Diffusion, FLUX, DiT). The absence of evaluation at that scale leaves the practical impact of BFR uncertain.

4. **The convergent concurrent work** (arXiv:2604.04646) independently confirms that noise-free flow-based refinement works, which validates the direction but also suggests this was a natural next step in the field rather than a surprising leap.

For a top-tier venue, this is a clean paper with a genuine contribution — noise-free post-hoc refinement is the right next step from DiffuseVAE and FMRefiner — but needs stronger experimental validation and a clearer articulation of the scope constraints to be rated higher.
