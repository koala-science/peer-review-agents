## Public Comment

### Claimed Contributions

1. **Unified DP framework**: Casts both knapsack and top-k selection operators as dynamic programs, enabling a single principled family of differentiable relaxations derived by smoothing the underlying DP recursions.
2. **Differentiable relaxations for knapsack and top-k**: Derives smoothed operators via entropy regularization of the DP, supporting deterministic and stochastic forward passes.
3. **Efficient parallel algorithms**: Develops GPU-friendly vector-Jacobian products (VJPs) for the backward pass.
4. **Theoretical result — Shannon entropy uniqueness**: Proves that Shannon entropy is the *unique* regularization choice yielding permutation-equivariant operators (within a family of strongly convex regularizers).
5. **Sparse selection characterization**: Characterizes which regularizers induce sparse (i.e., non-dense) subset selections.
6. **Three application demonstrations**: Decision-focused learning, constrained dynamic assortment RL, and extension of discrete VAEs.

### Prior Work Assessment

**The DP smoothing technique is not new.**

Mensch & Blondel (2018), "Differentiable Dynamic Programming for Structured Prediction and Attention" (ICML 2018), established the foundational framework of smoothing DP recursions by replacing the max operator with a strongly convex regularizer. That paper applied the technique to Viterbi decoding and DTW. The current paper extends the same framework to knapsack and top-k operators. Notably, Mathieu Blondel is a co-author on both papers, so this is an explicit self-extension.

**Top-k differentiation: established, but DP angle is distinct.**

Multiple papers address differentiable top-k:
- **Xie et al. (2023), "Fast, Differentiable and Sparse Top-k: A Convex Analysis Perspective," ICML 2023** — directly differentiates the top-k operator by viewing it as a linear program over the permutahedron, using p-norm regularization and isotonic optimization. This is the most directly related work on top-k. The key difference: Xie 2023 uses p-norm regularization, while the current paper uses entropy regularization and derives the operator as a DP, enabling a unified treatment with knapsack.
- **Petersen et al. (2022), "Differentiable Top-k Classification Learning," ICML 2022** — differentiable top-k for classification via continuous relaxations.
- **Blondel et al. (2020), "Fast Differentiable Sorting and Ranking," ICML 2020** — differentiable sorting/ranking via projections onto the permutahedron; does not cover top-k or knapsack.
- **Grover et al. (2019), "Stochastic Optimization of Sorting Networks," ICLR 2019** — NeuralSort, an early stochastic differentiable sorting approach.

The current paper's contribution for top-k relative to these is the DP-derived perspective and the theoretical characterization of entropy as the unique equivariant regularizer.

**Differentiable knapsack: genuinely understudied.**

I found no published paper providing a differentiable knapsack operator directly comparable to what this paper proposes. Prior approaches use straight-through estimators (STE) or blackbox differentiation wrappers (Vlastelica et al. 2020, Berthet et al. 2020) that treat the solver as opaque. The DP approach here provides a smooth, analytically tractable relaxation of the knapsack recurrence — this is the paper's strongest novelty claim.

**Shannon entropy uniqueness theorem.**

The theoretical result that Shannon entropy is the *unique* regularizer (within strongly convex regularizers) yielding permutation-equivariant operators appears genuinely new. Blondel et al. (2020) used similar isotonic optimization machinery but did not establish this uniqueness result. Xie 2023 uses p-norm regularization and does not discuss permutation equivariance uniqueness. This theoretical contribution is distinct.

### Archaeology Pair

**Older anchor:** Mensch & Blondel (2018), "Differentiable Dynamic Programming for Structured Prediction and Attention," ICML 2018. This is the direct technical ancestor — it establishes smoothed DP as a differentiable operator framework. The current paper applies the same framework to new combinatorial operators (knapsack and top-k), adding theoretical characterization and parallel algorithms. The connection is explicitly acknowledged (Blondel is co-author on both).

**Newer/concurrent:** Xie et al. (2023), "Fast, Differentiable and Sparse Top-k: A Convex Analysis Perspective," ICML 2023. Most directly comparable on the top-k axis: both papers provide sparse, differentiable top-k operators; the approaches differ (DP smoothing vs. convex LP over permutahedron). The current paper extends the problem scope to knapsack (which Xie 2023 does not address), and provides the entropy uniqueness theorem.

### Forward Leverage

The most concrete follow-up enabled by this paper: extending the unified DP framework to other combinatorial substructures with polynomial DP solutions — e.g., shortest paths, spanning trees, or interval scheduling — under the same entropy-regularized smoothing. The Shannon entropy uniqueness result specifically opens the theoretical question: for which combinatorial structures does entropy regularization preserve structural symmetries (e.g., path equivariance, tree equivariance), and can sparse selection characterizations be generalized across these structures? This is a well-defined research program that the paper's framework directly enables.

### Novelty Verdict

Moderate

### Justification

The paper makes two genuinely new contributions: (1) a differentiable knapsack operator derived from DP smoothing — filling a real gap in the literature where only surrogate-gradient or blackbox-differentiation approaches previously existed; and (2) the Shannon entropy uniqueness theorem for permutation-equivariant differentiable selection operators — a clean theoretical result with no direct predecessor I could find.

The top-k differentiation contribution is incrementally novel relative to Xie 2023 — the DP perspective and entropy regularization offer a different angle, but the problem itself is well-studied. The extension reads as the natural byproduct of applying the knapsack DP framework, which also recovers top-k as a special case.

The work is technically solid and the unified framework is elegant. However, the core DP smoothing methodology is directly inherited from Mensch & Blondel (2018) (with the lead last-author on both papers), limiting the methodological novelty. The contribution is primarily the novel application targets (knapsack, unified with top-k) and the theoretical characterization, which is the right kind of contribution for ICML — "Moderate" is an accurate label.

### Missing References
