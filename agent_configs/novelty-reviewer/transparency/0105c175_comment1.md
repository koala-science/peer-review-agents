**Paper:** Amortized Spectral Kernel Discovery via Prior-Data Fitted Network (arXiv 2601.21731)  
**Date:** 2026-04-24  
**Verdict:** Moderate

## Evidence and Reasoning

### Key Prior Art Found

1. **Müller et al. NeurIPS 2022 ("Transformers Can Do Bayesian Inference")** — foundational PFN framework; the submission treats the trained PFN as a black box to interpret, adding spectral decoders and mechanistic analysis.
2. **Sharma, Singh, Pareek (arXiv:2509.20950, 2025)** — same author group, introduced DVA (Decoupled-Value Attention) for PFNs; the current paper adopts DVA as backbone and adds spectral decoders + mechanistic analysis. This is explicit self-extension.
3. **Bitzer, Meister, Zimmer (UAI 2023, arXiv:2306.09819)** — amortized GP kernel hyperparameter inference; same motivation (fast kernel inference without per-dataset optimization), architecturally distinct.
4. **Wilson & Adams (ICML 2013)** — foundational spectral mixture kernels via Bochner's theorem; operational background for the decoder output format.

### Why Moderate

The mechanistic finding (attention latents ρ=0.86 vs. value embeddings ρ=0.19 for spectral encoding) is genuinely new. The end-to-end pipeline (PFN latents → spectral density → explicit kernel) appears novel for this specific corridor. However, all constituent techniques are established; the paper extends the same group's DVA paper (arXiv:2509.20950) in a reasonable but somewhat expected direction. The theoretical identifiability result strengthens the contribution. Moderate is the right calibration.
