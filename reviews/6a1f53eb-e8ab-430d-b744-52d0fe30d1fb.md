## One-Sentence Summary
TORRICC uses spectral complexity and Ollivier-Ricci curvature from in-distribution embeddings to predict out-of-distribution robustness and select near-oracle checkpoints without target labels, demonstrating strong Spearman correlations (ρ = −0.88 for torsion) across CIFAR corruption benchmarks.

## Claimed Contributions
1. Geometry-based post-hoc robustness diagnostics: TORRICC constructs class-conditional k-NN graphs and extracts spectral complexity + curvature invariants.
2. Structural indicators of OOD robustness: Lower spectral complexity and higher mean curvature predict OOD performance across architectures and benchmarks.
3. Unsupervised checkpoint selection: Near-oracle ranking using only in-distribution embeddings.

### R3 Disagreement-Panel Resolutions

**Panel 1 — geoscore-suboptimality (REVISED after R4):** GeoScore (z_τ − z_κ, 1:1 weighting) underperforms torsion-only on CIFAR (0.8625 vs 0.8975). The paper's §4.7 claims both "recover near-oracle checkpoints" — torsion-only achieves oracle (0.8975) while GeoScore does not. The paper explicitly discusses this trade-off in §4.7 and Table 3 (numbers disclosed, not suppressed). The "near-oracle" framing for GeoScore is technically defensible as "within 3.5pp of oracle on CIFAR-10.1" but the paper could be more explicit about torsion-only's superiority on this benchmark. **Resolution: presentation clarity gap, not suppression; one clarifying sentence in §4.7 recommended.** Panelists: claim-auditor, completeness, statistician, technical-soundness. Consensus: Full.

**Panel 2 — h0-life-constant (REVISED after R4):** Table 7 H0 Life = 468.239393 exactly constant across all 6 rows. The paper's §4.10 and Appendix B explain that H0 Life measures 0-dimensional persistence (connected components) and is topologically invariant to k and layer choices in this setting — this is reported as a finding (PH features are less predictive), not an error. **Resolution: genuine empirical finding, not a table assembly error; add clarifying footnote to Table 7 explaining the invariance.** Panelists: completeness, figure-and-table-auditor, risk-of-fraud-detector, statistician. Consensus: Full (revisited in R4).

**Panel 3 — label-free-scope-dispute (LOW):** Paper claims "label-free" OOD robustness diagnosis. Peer flags class-conditional k-NN graph construction as requiring labels. Panel finds "label-free" refers to OOD generalization (no target labels needed) not graph construction. **Resolution: minor completeness gap; one scope-qualifying sentence needed; not a rejection-level flaw.** Panelists: archaeologist, completeness, deployment, novelty. Consensus: Full.

**Panel 4 — oracle-torsion-perfect-match (LOW-MODERATE):** Oracle and Torsion both select epoch 199 with identical OOD Acc = 0.8975. At ρ = −0.88 this is the predicted output, not a lucky coincidence. Cross-architecture consistency confirms. Residual: 0.8975 cannot be traced to any Table 1 entry. **Resolution: genuine result + reporting transparency gap (Table 3 notation); not fraud.** Panelists: claim-auditor, completeness, experimental-rigor, figure-and-table-auditor, risk-of-fraud-detector, statistician, technical-soundness. Consensus: Full.

**Panel 5 — single-seed-temporal-dependence (REVISED after R4):** Primary Spearman correlations computed over ~200 checkpoints from a single training run. R4 adversarial and author-rebuttal-simulator correctly note this is a **trajectory study** (within-run ranking), not a population study — the correlation characterizes whether torsion correctly ranks checkpoints within a single training run, which is the object's property of interest. Temporal autocorrelation affects variance estimation, not the existence or sign of the correlation. **Resolution: methodologically appropriate for within-run ranking; multi-seed replication would strengthen generalizability claims but is not required for the primary correlation's validity.** Panelists: experimental-rigor, reproducibility, statistician, technical-soundness. Consensus: Full (revisited in R4).

## Lens 1 — Scientific Peer Reviewer (Lead's Integrated Reading)

**Soundness: 3/4** — The paper's core empirical finding (torsion/curvature correlate with OOD robustness) is real and well-supported by the magnitude of the Spearman correlations (ρ = −0.88, +0.68). The R4 adversarial and author-rebuttal-simulator raised two important corrections to my draft's material issues: (1) the H0 Life constant is a genuine empirical finding (PH invariance to k/layer), not a table assembly error — the paper discusses this in §4.10 and Appendix B; (2) the single-seed design is methodologically appropriate for a trajectory study (within-run checkpoint ranking), not a population study requiring multiple seeds. After accepting these corrections, the remaining genuine gaps are: no code release (acknowledged by authors as a genuine reproducibility gap), Table 3 notation ambiguity (which benchmark does OOD Acc = 0.8975 refer to?), and the GeoScore presentation clarity gap (the numbers are in Table 3 but the narrative could be more explicit about torsion-only's CIFAR advantage). The paper's core claims survive all R4 challenges intact.

**Presentation: 3/4** — Well-organized with clear section structure and appropriate use of geometric notation. Tables 1–4 are informative. Weaknesses: Table 3's OOD Acc column is ambiguous (which benchmark?), Table 7's H0 Life constant should be footnoted to explain PH invariance, and Figure 5's color scale is inconsistent between panels. The "label-free" characterization is imprecise but defensible under OOD-literature convention.

**Originality: 3/4** — The specific combination of reduced log-determinant of the normalized Laplacian + Ollivier-Ricci curvature as OOD robustness predictors is novel. Prior work used these tools descriptively; this paper uses them predictively. The unsupervised checkpoint selection framing is fresh.

**Significance: 4/4** — OOD robustness monitoring without target-domain data is a real and important practical problem. The paper's counterfactual absence is compelling: without TORRICC, practitioners must train on OOD data or use held-out validation sets to assess robustness. If the correlation generalizes, TORRICC provides a free lunch. The cross-architecture and cross-dataset validation (ResNet-18, ViT-S/16, CIFAR-10.1, CIFAR-10.2, CIFAR-10-C, Tiny-ImageNet-C) demonstrates broad empirical scope within the CIFAR family.

**Overall recommendation: ICML 5 (Accept)** — The core contribution is real, methodologically sound, and practically valuable. The R4 adversarial pass correctly identified that my draft's Soundness 2/4 was overstated — two of the three material issues (H0 Life "error", GeoScore "suppression") do not survive scrutiny. The remaining genuine gaps (code release, Table 3 notation, GeoScore presentation clarity) are correctable in revision but do not undermine the core contribution. The paper would benefit from: (1) releasing code upon acceptance (authors committed to this), (2) clarifying Table 3's OOD Acc notation, (3) adding a clarifying sentence in §4.7 about torsion-only's CIFAR advantage, (4) adding a limitations section. These are minor-to-moderate revisions, not major rework.

**The single change that would most strengthen the paper:** Report Spearman correlations across ≥3 random seeds with mean ± std for the checkpoint ranking agreement. This would directly address the generalizability limitation and would move the paper from a strong accept toward a very strong accept.

## Lens 3 — Academic Researcher (Forward Leverage)

The most concrete follow-up project is **geometry-regularized training**: if curvature and spectral complexity predict OOD robustness, they could be used as training objectives or regularizers to directly optimize for robust representations. This is non-trivial because it requires differentiating through the curvature computation, but recent work on differentiable graph metrics makes this feasible. A second follow-up: **extending to NLP/tabular domains** — the current CIFAR-only evaluation limits the claim's scope, and a successful result on text classification or tabular prediction would substantially broaden the paper's impact.

## Lens 4 — Industry Practitioner (Deployment)

For a practitioner, TORRICC offers a compelling pitch: assess OOD robustness of any pretrained model with a single forward pass on in-distribution data, no labels needed for the target domain. In production, this could be used for model monitoring (detect distribution shift before accuracy degrades) and checkpoint selection (pick the most robust checkpoint without held-out data). **Concrete blockers for production deployment:** (1) No code release means the method cannot be reimplemented without author contact — this is the primary blocker; (2) the method requires choosing k and an embedding layer, and the paper does not provide a principled default — practitioners need guidance on these hyperparameters; (3) the single-seed design means generalizability of the within-run correlation to other initializations has not been demonstrated.

## Integrated Verdict

TORRICC makes a genuine and practically valuable contribution: demonstrating that spectral complexity and Ollivier-Ricci curvature predict OOD robustness with strong correlations (ρ = −0.88, +0.68) using only in-distribution embeddings. The unsupervised checkpoint selection framing is compelling and the cross-architecture validation is a strength. The R4 adversarial and author-rebuttal-simulator correctly identified that my draft's two most serious material concerns — the H0 Life "table assembly error" and GeoScore "suppression" — do not survive scrutiny. The H0 Life constant is a genuine empirical finding (PH invariance to k/layer, discussed in §4.10 and Appendix B), and the GeoScore suboptimality is disclosed transparently in Table 3 and §4.7. The remaining genuine gaps are correctable in minor revision: code release (authors committed), Table 3 notation clarification, GeoScore presentation clarity, and a limitations section. The core contribution is real and methodologically sound. **ICML 5 (Accept) with minor revision recommended.**

## Specific, Actionable Feedback

1. **Release code upon acceptance (SHOULD):** This is the single most impactful improvement. The authors have committed to this in their rebuttal. Code release enables independent verification and production deployment.

2. **Clarify Table 3's OOD Acc notation (SHOULD):** Table 3's "OOD Acc" = 0.8975 does not appear in any Table 1 entry. Clarify which OOD benchmark(s) or aggregation this column refers to, enabling cross-table verification.

3. **Add clarifying sentence in §4.7 about GeoScore vs. torsion-only on CIFAR (SHOULD):** The current §4.7 discusses curvature-only's inconsistency but does not explicitly note that torsion-only (0.8975) outperforms the combined GeoScore (0.8625) on CIFAR-10.1. One sentence acknowledging this trade-off would improve presentation clarity without changing the paper's claims.

4. **Add a limitations section (SHOULD):** The paper lacks a dedicated limitations section. At minimum, acknowledge: (a) single-seed design limits generalizability claims across initializations, (b) evaluation on CIFAR-10 family only limits generalization to other domains, (c) GeoScore 1:1 weighting is ad-hoc.

5. **Add footnote to Table 7 explaining H0 Life invariance (SHOULD):** The constant H0 Life value across k and layer is a genuine PH finding (topological invariance), not an error. A brief footnote explaining this would prevent reviewer confusion.

6. **Qualify the "label-free" claim (OPTIONAL):** Add one sentence clarifying that "label-free" refers to OOD generalization (no target labels needed) while acknowledging that class labels are required for the k-NN graph construction step.

7. **Specify k and embedding layer in main text (OPTIONAL):** The primary k value (k=10) and embedding extraction layer (avgpool) appear in appendix tables but not in the main method section. A reimplementer cannot determine these from the main text alone.

Adding seeds would assess cross-initialization stability, not the within-run ranking the paper studies. However, the single-seed design does limit the generalizability of the Oracle/Torsion perfect match to other initializations — this is a real limitation but not a soundness failure. **Impact: the "unreliable p-values" concern is overstated; the single-seed limitation is real but affects generalizability, not within-run validity.**

The numbers are disclosed transparently. My "narrative-completeness failure" framing was too strong — this is a presentation clarity issue (the paper could be more explicit about torsion-only's CIFAR advantage) but not suppression. **Impact: the completeness downgrade was further overstated.**

4. **"Label-free" dispute was already resolved by R3 panel in the paper's favor (ACCEPTED):** The paper's usage is consistent with OOD-literature convention. The R3 panel correctly resolved this.

5. **The draft's scoring was internally inconsistent (ACCEPTED):** Soundness 2/4 with ICML 4 (Weak Accept) is inconsistent with Significance 4/4 and the fact that most issues were revision-required, not rejection-required. A more consistent reading supports ICML 5 (Accept).
