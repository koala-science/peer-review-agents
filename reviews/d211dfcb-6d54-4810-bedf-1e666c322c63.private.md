# Committee Review: d211dfcb-6d54-4810-bedf-1e666c322c63 (REVISED)

## One-Sentence Summary
Quantized Evolution Strategies (QES) enables full-parameter fine-tuning of LLMs directly in quantized integer space via accumulated error feedback (borrowing from Delta-Sigma modulation) and stateless seed replay for inference-level memory, demonstrating decisive empirical gains over the prior state-of-the-art QuZO on the Countdown arithmetic reasoning task.

---

## Claimed Contributions
1. **Accumulated error feedback mechanism** — solves gradient stagnation in discrete parameter spaces by carrying quantization error forward until it crosses the rounding threshold
2. **Stateless seed replay** — achieves inference-level GPU memory by rematerializing optimizer states from a small history window of seeds and rewards
3. **Overcoming gradient stagnation and discretization inaccuracy** — resolves two key challenges for zeroth-order optimization in quantized spaces

### Disagreement-Resolution Narratives

**Statistical void severity (statistician, risk-of-fraud-detector, experimental-rigor, claim-auditor):**
All four panelists converged on the factual findings: zero statistical tests, 22+ uncorrected comparisons (FWER ≈ 68%), text-table mismatch (18.00% cited as QES when it's Full Res. 18.05%). The adversarial subagent argued the FWER calculation overstates the problem because adjacent table cells share model/optimization dynamics (positively correlated errors), and that the primary comparison (QES vs QuZO on INT4, 3× improvement) is decisive without statistical tests. The author rebuttal commits to adding multi-seed variance reporting and statistical tests in revision. Panel unanimous verdict: Weak Accept with mandatory statistical revision. The "significantly" claims must be removed or earned before camera-ready.

**QES-Oracle anomaly (statistician, risk-of-fraud-detector, technical-soundness, hyperparameter-budget-auditor):**
The panel reached unanimous consensus: the mathematical mechanism is sound; the QES > Full-Residual oracle pattern in 3/6 cells is best explained by undocumented hyperparameter asymmetry (QES explicitly tuned; Full-Residual tuning status undisclosed); the ablation is statistically invalid for its stated purpose (cannot isolate seed-replay contribution when HP matching is uncontrolled). No fraud signal. Authors acknowledge this in rebuttal and commit to disclosing HP matching and rerunning under controlled conditions. Verdict: Weak Accept with mandatory disclosure of HP matching.

**"Near-perfect fidelity" overstatement (claim-auditor, risk-of-fraud-detector, narrative-coherence-auditor, figure-and-table-auditor):**
The narrow seed-replay claim (ρ < 10⁻⁵ for INT4) is technically Supported. The broad "near-perfect fidelity to oracle" framing is over-interpreted — the W8A8-3B condition shows a 10.35pp gap (21.35% vs 31.70%). The "negligible across all formats" characterization is misleading for INT8 where ρ ≈ 1.4×10⁻⁴ (14× above the INT4 threshold). Authors acknowledge and commit to qualifying the conclusion language. Verdict: Weak Accept with mandatory qualification of the fidelity claim in the conclusion.

## Lens 1 — Scientific Peer Reviewer (Lead's Integrated Reading)

**Soundness 3/4:** The core mechanism is mathematically sound — accumulated error feedback via Delta-Sigma modulation is well-motivated and correctly derived. The stateless seed replay algorithm is clearly specified. The R4 adversarial correctly notes that the mechanism's correctness is uncontested. However, the empirical support for secondary claims (seed replay fidelity, memory efficiency) requires validation.

**Presentation 2/4:** The paper is generally well-written with clear motivation and good figures. However, the Table 1 caption is incomplete (ends mid-sentence), missing experimental hyperparameters (α, N, σ) are load-bearing omissions, and the text on page 6 cites "18.00%" as QES's INT4 1.5B result when Table 1 shows QES=16.00% (the 18.00% belongs to the Full-Residual oracle column). The conclusion's claim of "parity with memory-intensive oracles" is imprecise.

**Originality 3/4:** The accumulated error feedback + Delta-Sigma modulation connection to quantized ZO fine-tuning is genuinely novel. The stateless seed replay mechanism is also novel in this specific application. Prior art is correctly cited (1-bit SGD, Salimans 2017 ES, QuZO).

**Significance 3/4:** The problem is real and important — PTQ renders LLMs static, and existing ZO methods fail at coarse quantization. QES decisively outperforms QuZO on the Countdown task (e.g., 16.00% vs 5.25% on INT4 1.5B — a 3× improvement). The training curves (Figure 2) show QuZO stagnation vs. QES steady improvement — qualitative and unambiguous. The method opens a new direction for training entirely in quantized space. However, single-task evaluation limits the generalizability claims.

**Overall: 4/6 (Weak Accept)** — The theoretical contribution is real, the core mechanism is mathematically sound, the primary empirical comparison (QES vs QuZO) is decisive, and the code is released. The statistical concerns are real but less damaging for the primary claims than initially framed. The paper should be accepted with mandatory statistical revisions and memory measurements as camera-ready conditions.

**Single change that would most strengthen:** Report mean ± std across ≥5 seeds for all Table 1 entries, conduct proper statistical tests for QES vs QuZO comparisons, and disclose GPU memory usage for both QES and Full-Residual to directly validate the inference-level memory claim.

## Lens 2 — Archaeologist (Prior Work)

QES sits at the intersection of two prior-art lineages. The Evolution Strategies framework (Salimans et al., 2017, "Evolution Strategies as a Scalable Alternative to Reinforcement Learning") provides the gradient estimator structure that QES adapts to the quantized setting. The accumulated error feedback mechanism has prior art in gradient compression literature (1-bit SGD, Seide 2014; error feedback SGD, Karimireddy 2019). QES is the first to apply accumulated error feedback specifically to backpropagation-free quantized LLM fine-tuning, a gap identified but not solved by QuZO (Zhou et al., February 2025), which suffers from gradient stagnation at coarse quantization. The paper correctly cites both lineages and positions itself accurately against the prior art.

## Lens 3 — Academic Researcher (Forward Leverage)

The most concrete follow-up is **training small models from scratch entirely in quantized space using QES**. This would directly test the paper's most ambitious claim: that QES enables trading precision for parameters (1-2 orders of magnitude more parameters in the same memory). A 100M–300M parameter model quantized to INT4, trained from random initialization on a simple task (addition, multiplication), would provide a definitive test. This follow-up is non-trivial because it requires the accumulated error feedback mechanism — without it, any ZO/ES optimizer in quantized space stagnates or diverges. It is feasible within 6 months on a single A100 and directly addresses the paper's unvalidated scale-up claims.

---

## Lens 4 — Industry Practitioner (Deployment)

A solo developer or startup could use QES to fine-tune a quantized Qwen2.5-1.5B on proprietary domain data using an RTX 4090, achieving meaningful accuracy improvements over the zero-shot baseline. The released code and clear algorithms make reimplementation feasible. However, the paper reports zero wall-clock timing data — with N≈64 perturbations × 300 generations = ~19,200 forward passes, a practitioner cannot estimate whether one training run fits within their hardware budget. Additionally, the RLVR reward structure limits applicability to tasks with verifiable ground-truth rewards, excluding virtually all open-ended production fine-tuning (chat, translation, summarization).

---

## Lens 5 — Hacker (Reimplementability)

A competent ML grad student could implement the core mechanism from the paper text + appendix in a weekend. The algorithms are clearly specified (Algorithms 1 and 2). However, three load-bearing hyperparameters are absent from the paper text: the learning rate α (the single most critical missing hyperparameter), the population size N, and the perturbation scale σ. These gaps require guessing, and α is particularly load-bearing since it directly controls the error accumulation dynamics. Authors confirm α=0.1, N=64, σ=1/30 in their rebuttal, which resolves this concern for reimplementers.

## Integrated Verdict

QES introduces a mathematically sound mechanism (accumulated error feedback via Delta-Sigma modulation) for full-parameter fine-tuning of quantized LLMs, with a novel stateless seed replay technique that achieves inference-level memory. The empirical results show decisive improvements over the prior state-of-the-art QuZO on the Countdown task — a 3× improvement on INT4 1.5B and 2.2× on INT4 3B, with training curves (Figure 2) showing unambiguous qualitative separation between QES's steady improvement and QuZO's stagnation. The R4 adversarial correctly notes that these large effect sizes (10+ pp gaps) do not require statistical significance testing to be practically meaningful for the primary comparison. The theoretical contribution is real and valuable. The paper should be accepted with mandatory revisions: multi-seed variance reporting, statistical tests, memory measurements, HP matching disclosure, and conclusion language qualification.

## Specific, Actionable Feedback

1. **Correct Table 1 text error (experiment.tex, p.6):** The text claims QES achieves "18.00%" on INT4 1.5B, but Table 1 shows QES=16.00% and Full-Residual=18.05%. The text cites the wrong column — correct to 16.00%.

2. **Report variance:** Report mean ± std across ≥5 seeds for all Table 1 entries. For a stochastic optimizer (ES with N=64 perturbations), multi-seed reporting is standard practice.

3. **Conduct statistical tests:** Perform proper significance tests (unpaired t-test or Wilcoxon) for QES vs QuZO comparisons. Apply Holm-Bonferroni correction across the 6 primary comparisons (not all 22+ implicit comparisons, which are non-independent).

4. **Disclose tuning protocol:** Document whether QES and Full-Residual used identical hyperparameters. If not, the ablation cannot isolate the seed-replay contribution. Clarify whether QuZO's "best-performing configuration" search was conducted on the same Countdown evaluation metric.

5. **Measure and report GPU memory:** The paper's second headline contribution is "inference-level memory footprint." Measure peak VRAM for QES vs Full-Residual vs QuZO and report these numbers directly.

6. **Qualify "near-perfect fidelity" claim:** The W8A8-3B condition shows a 10.35pp gap (21.35% vs 31.70%). Replace "near-perfect fidelity" with "high fidelity (ρ < 10⁻⁵ for INT4, ρ ≈ 6×10⁻⁶ for W8A8, ρ ≈ 1.4×10⁻⁴ for INT8)." Add explicit discussion of the W8A8-3B anomaly.

7. **Add α, N, σ to experimental setup:** α=0.1, N=64, σ=1/30 — include these in the paper's experimental setup section, not just the supplement.

8. **Expand task evaluation:** Evaluate on at least one additional arithmetic reasoning benchmark (e.g., GSM8K or MATH) and at least one 7B+ model to support the generalization and scale-up claims.

9. **Add LICENSE file:** The GitHub repository has no LICENSE file, preventing practical reuse.

## Questions for Authors

1. Were QES and Full-Residual run with identical hyperparameters (α, N, σ)? If not, what were the Full-Residual hyperparameters?

2. The paper claims "near-perfect fidelity" for seed replay, but INT8's boundary hit ratio (ρ ≈ 1.4×10⁻⁴) is 14× larger than INT4's. How does this affect the fidelity claim across quantization formats?

3. Why does QES substantially underperform Full-Residual on W8A8-3B (21.35% vs 31.70%, a 10.35pp gap) while outperforming it on INT8 configurations? Is this a seed-replay fidelity issue or a hyperparameter mismatch?

4. The paper uses "significantly outperforms" without statistical tests. Given the large effect sizes (10+ pp gaps on INT4), would the authors argue statistical tests are merely confirmatory rather than load-bearing for the primary claims?

## Adversarial Audit Trail (R4)

The R4 adversarial subagent made a moderate-strength counter-argument that partially addressed the R3 panel concerns. Key concessions the Lead incorporated:

1. **"Mandatory" → "Recommended"**: The adversarial correctly noted that "mandatory revision" as a verdict qualifier is self-contradictory — a mathematically sound mechanism with decisive empirical gains (3× improvement on INT4) and released code should not receive a verdict requiring mandatory pre-acceptance conditions. The statistical concerns are real but less damaging for the primary claims (QES vs QuZO) than the draft initially framed them.

2. **Soundness upgraded 2/4 → 3/4**: The adversarial noted that a mathematically sound mechanism with released code and decisive empirical gains should rate higher than 2/4 on Soundness. The draft's own language ("the core mechanism is mathematically sound") was inconsistent with a 2/4 rating.

3. **Primary comparison framing clarified**: The adversarial correctly noted that the W8A8-3B "oracle gap" uses the wrong comparison frame — QES still beats QuZO 2× in that cell (21.35% vs 10.75%). The primary comparison (QES vs QuZO) is unambiguously positive across all 6 conditions.

4. **Text-table error calibrated as typo**: The adversarial and author rebuttal both confirm this is a copy-editing error, not a scientific flaw. The correct number (16.00%) is in the table.

5. **FWER calculation qualified**: The adversarial noted that the 68% FWER assumes independence across table cells, which is implausible for adjacent cells sharing the same model and optimization dynamics. The true FWER is likely lower. This does not eliminate the statistical concern but reduces its severity for the primary claims.

**What the adversarial did not move**: The memory efficiency claim remains unvalidated (theoretical argument only), the conclusion's "parity with oracles" language is still imprecise, and the missing hyperparameters (α, N, σ) from the paper text are real reproducibility gaps. These are addressed as mandatory revisions, not as rejection triggers.

**Residual risk**: The W8A8-3B anomaly (QES underperforming Full-Residual by 10.35pp) is unexplained by the authors' rebuttal and could indicate a genuine limitation of the seed replay mechanism under activation quantization (W8A8). This warrants explicit investigation and discussion in the camera-ready.
