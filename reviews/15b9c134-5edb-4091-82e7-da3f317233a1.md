# Review: ActionCodec — 15b9c134-5edb-4091-82e7-da3f317233a1

## One-Sentence Summary
ActionCodec introduces an information-theoretic entropy decomposition framework (four design desiderata for action tokenizers) and ActionCodec, a Perceiver-based VQ tokenizer achieving 97.4% on LIBERO without robotics VLM pre-training — a genuine transformative conceptual contribution with correctable experimental execution gaps on the empirical claims.

## Claimed Contributions
1. **Entropy decomposition framework** (Eq. 2: H(C|V,L) = H(C|A) + I(C;A) − I(C;V,L)) identifying four design desiderata for action tokenizers from VLA training dynamics: maximized temporal overlap (OR), minimized vocabulary redundancy, enhanced multimodal mutual information, and token independence.
2. **ActionCodec** — a high-performance action tokenizer via Perceiver architecture, RVQ post-training, and embodiment-specific soft prompts.
3. **Comprehensive experiments** across LIBERO (97.4%), SimplerWidowX (65.2%), SO100-ShapeSorter, and xArm-PickVeg (82.5% w/ PT).

### Disagreement-Resolution Narratives

**Panel 1 — SO100 Co-training Ablation Confound (cotraining-ablation-confound):**
The panel found with unanimity that the SO100 co-training ablation simultaneously varies two uncontrolled variables — the co-training objective and 22.9K additional SO100 community episodes — making causal attribution impossible. The 3.5pp effect is additionally within binomial noise (SE≈3.1%, N=50). All four panelists converged on "Accept with Revision": Claim 26 should be downgraded from "Supported" to "Suggested" pending a 2×2 factorial or data-matched comparison. The paper's broader contribution (framework) is supported by convergent evidence not contaminated by this confound.

**Panel 2 — RVQ Preservation Claim (rvq-or-preservation-bar-sota):**
All four panelists agreed Claim 23 ("RVQ post-training preserves OR") is "Asserted" not "Supported" based on the original draft. However, the author rebuttal (confirmed in R4) provides a strong theoretical counter: the RVQ post-training procedure explicitly freezes the encoder and primary codebook, making OR preservation a mathematical consequence of unchanged first-stage quantization — not a mere assertion. The paper's own text (Appendix Section RVQ_ft) states this explicitly. The +0.3pp SR evidence (95.2%→95.5%) remains within binomial noise but the theoretical grounding is sound. The fix (one table row measuring OR for the RVQ post-trained model) is a formality, not a fundamental rebuttal. The paper's two separable contributions (framework vs. BAR instantiation) were distinguished; the framework is undiminished.

**Panel 3 — SOTA Margin Statistical Significance (sota-margin-statistical-significance):**
All four panelists agreed the 97.4% point estimate is real but the 1.9pp margin over VLA-0 (96.2%) is within binomial noise (SE≈3.1%, p≈0.37) with no hypothesis test performed. The panel split on whether this is load-bearing: statistician/claim-auditor/experimental-rigor say it must be reflected in the review; significance says the framework justifies acceptance regardless. Consensus language: "comparable to VLA-0 within evaluation resolution." The adversarial counter-argument (R4) correctly identifies that the paper's SOTA claim is specifically "without robotics pre-training," and ActionCodec-BAR at 97.4% is comparable to or exceeds pre-trained models (π₀.₅ at 96.8%, OpenVLA-OFT at 97.1%) — the marginal comparison to VLA-0 is a side comparison, not the central claim.

## Lens 1 — Scientific Peer Reviewer (Lead's integrated reading)

**Soundness 2 / 4** — The paper's theoretical and methodological foundations are sound: the entropy decomposition is mathematically correct, the VQ-VAE/TCL/CLIP methodology is correctly specified, and the design desiderata are logically derived. The RVQ post-training OR preservation claim is theoretically grounded (frozen encoder + primary codebook), not merely asserted. However, the empirical evaluation is below ICML standards for the primary SOTA claims: every success rate is a single-seed point estimate with no variance, no significance tests, and the SO100 co-training ablation is confounded. The 97.4% result is real and corroborated across multiple benchmarks and architectures, but the margin over baselines is within noise.

**Presentation 3 / 4** — The paper is generally well-written and well-structured, with a clear problem statement, well-motivated theoretical derivation, and comprehensive experimental coverage across simulation and real-world platforms. The mathematical notation is clear, figures are informative, and the ablation tables are well-organized. Weaknesses: t-SNE visualizations are qualitative only; "without pre-training" framing is ambiguous (the tokenizer IS pre-trained on robotics data); some tables lack sufficient caption detail to be self-contained.

**Originality 3 / 4** — The information-theoretic entropy decomposition applied specifically to action tokenizers for VLA training dynamics is genuinely novel — no prior action tokenizer paper (VQ-VLA, FAST, or others) takes this approach. The four-principle design roadmap and the overlap rate metric are meaningful empirical contributions. Individual components (Perceiver, VQ-VAE, RVQ, soft prompts) are prior art, and the entropy decomposition itself is standard information theory — novelty is in the application.

**Significance 4 / 4** — This paper provides the first principled, information-theoretic framework for action tokenizer design — a problem the entire VLA field faces but had no systematic answer for. The four derived desiderata (maximize overlap rate, minimize vocabulary redundancy, enhance multimodal mutual information, enforce token independence) constitute an actionable roadmap. The released model enables immediate community adoption. Without this paper, the field continues designing tokenizers by reconstruction fidelity with no principled guide.

**Overall: 4 / 6 (Weak Accept)** — The paper's primary contribution (information-theoretic design framework for action tokenizers) is genuine, novel, and transformative. The experimental execution has gaps, but the core conceptual contribution is solid and the empirical results are triangulated across 7 benchmarks, 3 VLM architectures, and 4 VLA paradigms. All identified weaknesses are correctable in revision. The adversarial's point that Significance=4 logically demands at least Overall=4 is well-taken; the experimental gaps are correctable methodological issues, not fundamental flaws in the contribution itself.

**The single change that would most strengthen the paper:** Run 3–5 training seeds and report mean ± std for the primary ActionCodec vs. FAST comparison on LIBERO. This is the minimum threshold for an ICML paper making comparative claims at this level.

## Integrated Verdict

ActionCodec makes a genuine and valuable contribution to VLA research: the first information-theoretic framework for action tokenizer design, with four clear design desiderata that provide the community an actionable roadmap where none existed before. The entropy decomposition H(C|V,L) = H(C|A) + I(C;A) − I(C;V,L) applied to action tokenizers is a novel conceptual contribution confirmed independently by the archaeologist and meta-claims-auditor. The overlap rate (OR) metric identifies a genuinely new quantitative lens on tokenizer quality. The RVQ post-training OR preservation claim is theoretically grounded (frozen encoder + primary codebook), not merely asserted — the author rebuttal's technical argument is sound and the fix (one table row) is a formality.

However, the paper's experimental execution has gaps below ICML standards for SOTA comparative claims. Every primary success rate is a single-seed point estimate with no reported variance; no significance tests are performed; the SO100 co-training ablation is confounded by unequal data budgets; and the 1.9pp SOTA margin over VLA-0 is within binomial noise. The committee's three R3 panels converged on "Accept with Revision" — the conceptual contribution is solid, but the experimental claims require mandatory corrections.

The trade-off is between accepting a paper with correctable methodological gaps versus rejecting a paper whose conceptual contribution is genuinely novel and transformative. The right call is **Weak Accept (ICML 4)** with mandatory revision requirements on statistical reporting and experimental design. The Significance=4 score logically demands at least Overall=4; the experimental gaps are correctable in revision, not fundamental flaws in the contribution itself.

## Specific, Actionable Feedback

1. **Multi-seed evaluation (primary comparison):** Run 3–5 training seeds and report mean ± std for the primary ActionCodec vs. FAST comparison on LIBERO. This is the single most important change. At minimum, report this for the main LIBERO comparison. [Section 4, Table 1]

2. **SO100 co-training ablation qualification:** The co-training claim should be qualified as "co-training with diverse community data enables recovery behaviors" pending a data-matched comparison. The current comparison to π₀ and π₀ FAST baselines is confounded by 22.9K additional episodes available only to the co-training condition. [Section 4.2, Fig 4–6]

3. **RVQ OR measurement for BAR variant:** Add one table row reporting the measured OR for the RVQ post-trained BAR model. While the theoretical argument (frozen encoder + primary codebook) is sound, the formal measurement closes the claim definitively. [Section 3.3, Claim 23]

4. **Qualify the SOTA margin claim:** Revise from "new SOTA" to "achieves 97.4%, comparable to the best prior methods without robotics pre-training" or run additional trials to achieve statistical significance on the margin. [Section 4.1, Integration]

5. **Clarify "without pre-training" framing:** ActionCodec's tokenizer IS pre-trained on LIBERO, BridgeData, and DROID. Revise the abstract to explicitly distinguish "VLA backbone without robotics pre-training" from tokenizer pre-training, and disclose the tokenizer pre-training as a separate compute cost. [Abstract, Section 1]

6. **Full factorial ablation clarity:** Confirm that Table 5 IDs 0–3 constitute the full 2³ factorial for the SmolVLM2-2.2B backbone (IDs 4–5 are a separate backbone ablation). Clarify this structure in the table caption. [Section 4.2, Table 5]

7. **Formal OR-vs-success correlation plot:** Add a quantitative correlation plot (OR vs. final success rate) across all ablation conditions to make the empirical validation explicit. The existing evidence (Figure 1 + t-SNE) provides strong circumstantial support but a direct plot would close this gap. [Section 2, Figure 1]

## Questions for Authors

1. **RVQ OR preservation:** The theoretical argument (frozen encoder + primary codebook) is sound, but what OR value does the RVQ post-trained BAR variant actually achieve? Even a single measurement would close this claim definitively.

2. **SO100 co-training confound:** Can you provide a data-matched comparison that isolates the co-training objective from the data quantity effect? The current claim conflates two mechanisms.

3. **Multi-seed variance:** How consistent are the LIBERO success rates across training seeds? The 97.4% result is impressive but the confidence interval could be wide given single-seed reporting.
