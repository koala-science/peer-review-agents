## Raw committee centroid vs. final verdict

The unweighted committee centroid was approximately 3.0 (Weak Reject/Weak Accept boundary). The final verdict of 4/6 is at the upper edge of the committee range, justified by:
- R3 panels partially resolving contested findings in the paper's favor (Kalman framing = completeness not soundness; data efficiency = disclosed; memory ablation = directionally supportive)
- Strong author rebuttal on the Kalman framing (Sigmoid constraint explicitly defines diagonal case)
- Core technical claims (Bayesian decomposition, KDE-attention equivalence) are mathematically sound

The ~1-point upward shift from centroid is defensible but close to the boundary. A strictly by-the-numbers committee would likely land at 3/6.

---

## Hedges and residual uncertainties

1. **Statistical reporting:** The paper has zero variance/significance testing throughout. The "large margins" argument is reasonable but unverified. The calibrator flagged this as the top concern.

2. **10% data causal attribution:** The NE comparison (NeuroKalman 10% vs. TravelUAV 100%) is disclosed but causally ambiguous — without a 10%-data backbone-without-Kalman baseline, the Kalman mechanism's specific contribution cannot be isolated from full-data pretraining transfer. The R3 panel flagged this and the paper should add this control.

3. **Kalman gain framing:** The mechanism is mathematically sound as element-wise diagonal fusion (consistent with KalmanNet), but the section headings and abstract use "Kalman Gain" language that implies classical optimal gain. This is a real clarity/completeness concern that the authors should address via nomenclature revision.

4. **Theoretical analysis:** The "error contraction" bound (Appendix Eq. 21–26) requires unverified λ_GRU assumptions. It demonstrates boundedness, not guaranteed error reduction. The paper overstates this contribution.

5. **MBGRU ablation coverage:** The review revised this to note MBGRU covers all 3 test splits (not just one). This makes the memory-pathway evidence stronger than initially characterized.

6. **TravelUAV benchmark protocol:** The benchmark does not mandate multi-seed reporting, so the variance concern reflects ICML norms rather than a benchmark protocol violation. This contextualizes (1) above.

## Findings not in public review

- Statistician subagent flagged potential auxiliary loss ablation copy-paste duplication (statistician R2 Finding 1) — elevated to R4 Audit Trail as a footnote rather than a direct feedback item. Authors should clarify.
- TravelUAV-FT degradation anomaly: 10% fine-tuning actually hurts vs. full-data TravelUAV (17.56% vs. 16.10% SR). This is mentioned in the technical-soundness R1 but not discussed in the public review. It provides additional context for why the 10% data result is non-trivial — the Kalman mechanism appears to protect against fine-tuning degradation that would otherwise occur.
- The hyperparameter-budget-auditor noted NavFoM uses a fundamentally different pre-training paradigm (foundation model pre-training) vs. TravelUAV-FT (task-specific fine-tuning). This makes the NavFoM comparison a different kind of baseline entirely, not directly comparable under the 10% data constraint. The review captures this but the point is worth emphasizing.
