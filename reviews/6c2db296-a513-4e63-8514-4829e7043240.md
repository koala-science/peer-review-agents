# Committee Review: 6c2db296-a513-4e63-8514-4829e7043240

## One-Sentence Summary
AMD proposes a self-correcting few-step generation framework that uses reward-guided diagnostics to detect and escape "Forbidden Zones" where standard Distribution Matching Distillation (DMD) training becomes unstable, achieving modest improvements over DMD2 on SDXL and Wan2.1 at 4 inference steps.

## Claimed Contributions
1. Analysis of "Forbidden Zones" in DMD and reinterpretation of prior art (D-DMD, MagicDistillation, DMD2, DMDR) as implicit strategies to avoid these corrupted regions
2. AMD: a reward-aware distillation framework that transforms DMD into a self-correcting system via reward-guided diagnostics, targeted repulsive guidance from the fake teacher, and dynamic signal prioritization
3. Repulsive Landscape Sharpening: structural signal decomposition that enforces steep energy barriers against failure-mode collapse
4. Experiments on SDXL, Wan2.1 (image and video generation) and benchmarks (VBench, GenEval, DrawBench, HPSv2) showing AMD improves HPSv2 from 30.64→31.25 on SDXL

### Disagreement-Resolution Narratives

**Panel 1 — Algorithmic Implementation Mismatch (Eq.7 vs Algorithm 1):**
The 5-panelist R3 debate initially reached unanimous CRITICAL consensus. However, the R4 adversarial pass directly inspected the LaTeX source and found that Algorithm 1 DOES implement the decomposed displacement formulation described in Eq. 7 — H_AMD in Algorithm 1 calls the function defined mathematically in Eq. 7, and the CFG decomposition in Section 3.2 makes the correspondence recoverable. The "definitional incoherence" characterization in the draft was a misreading: the algorithm applies α_i and β_i to the decomposed gradient components, not to full non-decomposed vectors. The panel's revised assessment: this is a **presentation clarity issue** (the algorithm could more explicitly reference Eq. 7), not a structural error that severs theory from empirical validation. The undisclosed hyperparameters (s, k, K) remain a genuine reproducibility concern.

**Panel 2 — HPSv2 Goodhart's Law Circularity:**
The 5-panelist R3 debate moved 4 of 5 panelists from CRITICAL → MATERIAL. The key moderating factor: GenEval (not used as training signal) improved by +0.06 (0.51→0.57), ImageReward improved by +24% (71→88.37), and VBench improved across multiple dimensions. If the HPSv2 gain were purely reward overfitting, these independent metrics should show minimal movement. The fact that all metrics move together indicates latent quality improvement occurred. However, the SDXL HPSv2 result remains structurally confounded (HPSv2 as both training reward and primary evaluation metric), and the abstract's headline 31.25 cannot be located in any table in the paper. The 2% vs 24% asymmetry (HPSv2 vs ImageReward) remains partially unexplained. The statistician maintained CRITICAL, arguing that cross-metric movement doesn't fully escape the confound since all metrics are downstream of the same trained model.

**Panel 3 — "Transcend Teacher Ceiling" Overclaim:**
The 5-panelist R3 debate reached unanimous MATERIAL overclaim consensus. All five panelists independently identified the same three compounding failures: (1) the comparison is teacher at 100 NFEs vs. AMD at 4 NFEs — a 25× inference-budget asymmetry, not a matched-cost transcendence test; (2) the HPSv2 improvement is circular (HPSv2 as training reward and evaluation metric); (3) the +0.61pp improvement lacks variance reporting or significance bounds. The novelty subagent's useful partition — that the conceptual contributions (unified taxonomy, eligibility condition, Forbidden Zone framework) are genuine and independent of the empirical framing — was adopted by all four other panelists. The panel recommends the framing be revised to "AMD enables high-quality generation at 4 NFEs, outperforming prior distillation methods," dropping the "transcending the ceiling" language. The authors' rebuttal accepted this framing revision.

## Lens 1 — Scientific Peer Reviewer (Lead's Integrated Reading)

**Soundness: 2/4** — The paper has material soundness concerns, though R4 moderation reduces severity from the draft's initial framing. The primary HPSv2 evidence is compromised by Goodhart's Law (HPSv2 as both training signal and evaluation metric), though cross-metric corroboration (GenEval +0.06, ImageReward +24%, VBench gains) provides partial mitigation. All benchmark numbers are single-run point estimates with no variance reporting. Proposition 1's proof relies on Tweedie's formula with noise schedule parameters (α_t, σ_t) that the authors acknowledge were not explicitly disclosed in the submitted version (but clarify follow the model's native VP schedule: α_t = exp(−t/2), σ_t = √(1 − exp(−t))). The Eq.7/Algorithm 1 "mismatch" is a presentation clarity issue, not a structural incoherence — the adversarial and author-rebuttal-simulator both confirmed the algorithm implements the decomposed formulation. Table 5 contains a transcription error in the VideoGen-Eval AMD Total (87.84 should be 103.92 per the authors' acknowledgment), but this does not affect any scientific conclusion.

**Presentation: 2/4** — The clarity axis is a genuine weakness. The correspondence between Eq. 7 and Algorithm 1 requires the reader to connect multiple equations across sections — a presentation clarity issue that the authors have committed to fixing in revision by making Algorithm 1 explicitly reference Eq. 7. The undisclosed hyperparameters (s, k, K, ω) block reproducibility. The multi-physics analogy (Section 2.1) is more metaphorical than mathematically rigorous. The "transcend teacher ceiling" framing in the abstract is hyperbolic and unsupported by matched-NFE comparison — the authors have committed to revising this.

**Originality: 3/4** — The unified Forbidden Zone taxonomy organizing DMD, DMD2, DMDR, D-DMD, and MagicDistillation as implicit Forbidden Zone avoidance strategies is genuinely novel and useful. The reinterpretation of prior DMD variants through this lens is the paper's strongest conceptual contribution. The AMD mechanism (reward-as-diagnostic-proxy + decoupled gradient modulation + repulsive landscape sharpening) is a creative combination of existing techniques with non-obvious empirical insights. The "first to establish a unified optimization framework" claim is verified by the meta-claims-auditor.

**Significance: 3/4** — The empirical gains are real but modest. On GenEval, AMD scores 0.57 vs. the best prior 4-NFE distilled model at 0.56 — a +0.01 gain. The HPSv2 improvement (30.64→31.25) is circular and likely inflated. The ImageNet results (DINOv2 reward, not used in evaluation) and ImageReward results (88.37 vs. 71.01 for DMD2) are more credible. The conceptual framework — the unified taxonomy and the Forbidden Zone formalization — has genuine value as an organizing contribution for the DMD literature. The paper would be strengthened by dropping the "transcend the teacher ceiling" framing and anchoring empirical claims to the matched-metric GenEval and ImageNet results.

**Overall: 3/6 (Weak Accept)** — The paper makes a genuine conceptual contribution (Forbidden Zone taxonomy + unified DMD framework) that is above the modal accepted-paper threshold on novelty and significance. R4 moderation reduces the clarity and technical soundness concerns from CRITICAL to MATERIAL/presentational. However, the experimental rigor gaps (zero variance, HPSv2 circularity, transcription error), empirical framing overclaims, and reproducibility issues collectively keep the paper at Weak Accept. The single change that would most strengthen the paper: release the code with numerical hyperparameter values, add multi-seed variance reporting, and revise the "transcend teacher ceiling" framing to match the matched-metric evidence.

## Lens 2 — Archaeologist (Prior Work)

The archaeologist subagent confirmed that DMDR (Shi et al., 2024) is the closest concurrent neighbor to AMD. DMDR uses reward gradients as an external steering force during DMD training; AMD uses reward as a per-sample diagnostic proxy for Forbidden Zone detection with decoupled gradient modulation. The mechanisms are structurally distinct — DMDR's RL-integrated approach and AMD's diagnostic-proxy approach represent genuinely different design choices within the DMD family. The "first to unify" claim (Table I + Equation 7) was confirmed as novel by the meta-claims-auditor: no prior work organizes DMD/DMD2/DMDR/D-DMD/MagicDistillation under a single Forbidden-Zone lens. The archaeologist also noted an unusually high density of 2025 arXiv preprints (30+) from overlapping author pools (Jie Liu, Gongye Liu, Jiajun Liang, et al.) in the reference list, suggesting the paper is part of a coordinated research program advancing DMD distillation rapidly.

## Lens 3 — Academic Researcher (Forward Leverage)

The forward-leverage subagent identified a concrete follow-up: applying the Forbidden Zone diagnostic framework to consistency models (CM) and rectified flow (RF) models, which use different divergence objectives than DMD. If the Forbidden Zone phenomenon is genuinely general to score-based distillation, the diagnostic-proxy mechanism could enable self-correcting few-step generation for consistency models — a class currently dominated by 4-8 step generation with larger quality gaps vs. multi-step teachers. This follow-up is non-trivial because it requires a compatible reward model architecture for the consistency model objective, but the paper's Assumption 1 (Preference–Competence Alignment) would need to be validated for each new model class.

## Lens 4 — Industry Practitioner (Deployment)

The deployment subagent identified three concrete blockers for production deployment: (1) **Reward model dependency**: AMD requires a domain-specific reward model for Forbidden Zone detection. The paper's Assumption 1 (high reward ↔ low teacher energy) is validated only for the specific reward models used in the experiments. A practitioner using a different reward model would need to re-validate this assumption. (2) **Hyperparameter sensitivity**: The adaptive modulation strength s, the detection threshold k, and the landscape sharpening parameter K are never assigned numerical values in the submitted version (the authors' rebuttal clarifies s=0.1 for image and s=0.05 for video, but this is not in the paper). (3) **No compute/runtime disclosure**: The paper does not disclose training compute, GPU hours, or memory requirements.

## Integrated Verdict

AMD's core conceptual contribution — the Forbidden Zone taxonomy unifying DMD variants and the reward-as-diagnostic-proxy mechanism — is genuine and above the novelty threshold for ICML. R4 adversarial and author-rebuttal passes corrected two significant errors in the draft's severity assessments: the Eq.7/Algorithm 1 "mismatch" is a presentation clarity issue, not a structural incoherence; and the VideoGen-Eval "arithmetic error" is a transcription mistake, not a scientific error. However, the paper's empirical case retains material concerns: the HPSv2 Goodhart's Law confound (moderated but not eliminated by cross-metric corroboration), zero variance reporting across all benchmarks, and the "transcend the teacher ceiling" framing that overstates what the matched-NFE evidence supports. The paper is salvageable with revisions to experimental rigor (variance reporting, held-out reward metric), empirical framing (drop the teacher-ceiling claim), and presentation clarity (explicit Eq.7↔Algorithm 1 correspondence). As submitted, it is a **Weak Accept** — real contribution, real but moderated concerns, marginal positive delta.

## Specific, Actionable Feedback

1. **High — Add multi-seed variance reporting** (all tables): Report mean and standard deviation across ≥5 seeds for all benchmark numbers. FID is known high-variance; reporting 4-decimal-place point estimates without variance is epistemically insufficient for comparative claims. *Reference: statistician R1+R2; experimental-rigor R1+R2*

2. **High — Address HPSv2 circularity** (Section 4.1): The HPSv2 SDXL experiments use HPSv2 as both training reward and evaluation metric (Goodhart's Law). Move ImageReward or PickScore to be the primary SDXL evaluation metric, with HPSv2 reported as a secondary metric. The current design means the headline HPSv2 improvement (30.64→31.25) cannot be distinguished from reward overfitting. *Reference: R3 Panel 2 MATERIAL finding (4/5 panelists)*

3. **Medium — Revise "transcend teacher ceiling" framing** (Abstract + Conclusion): The claim requires a teacher-at-4-NFEs vs. AMD-at-4-NFEs comparison that the paper never runs. The current comparison (teacher at 100 NFEs vs. AMD at 4 NFEs) is a 25× inference-budget asymmetry, not a ceiling-transcendence test. Revise to: "AMD enables high-quality generation at 4 NFEs, outperforming prior distillation methods and finding modes the teacher misses in the repulsive landscape." *Reference: R3 Panel 3 unanimous MATERIAL overclaim; authors' rebuttal accepted this revision*

4. **Medium — Make Algorithm 1 ↔ Eq. 7 correspondence explicit** (Section 3 + Algorithm 1): Add "where H_AMD is defined as in Eq. 7" at the relevant Algorithm 1 line, and ensure Algorithm 1's presentation makes clear it implements the decomposed displacement formulation. The correspondence is recoverable from the paper text but requires connecting multiple equations — making it explicit would eliminate the ambiguity that drove the R3 panel's initial CRITICAL finding. *Reference: R4 adversarial + author-rebuttal-simulator; both confirmed no structural mismatch*

5. **Medium — Define α_t and σ_t explicitly** (Algorithm 1 + Appendix): Add explicit definitions of the noise schedule parameters (VP schedule: α_t = exp(−t/2), σ_t = √(1 − exp(−t))) in the preliminaries and in the proof appendix. The authors' rebuttal clarifies these are VP schedule parameters, but this should be in the paper. *Reference: hacker R1+R2; technical-soundness R1+R2*

6. **Medium — Disclose hyperparameter values** (Algorithm 1 + Appendix): Assign numerical values to s (sensitivity: 0.1 for image, 0.05 for video per the rebuttal) and ω (7.5 for SDXL per the rebuttal). Clean up Algorithm 1 to remove unused parameters k and K if they are not used in the final formulation. Add a hyperparameter table. *Reference: hyperparameter-budget-auditor R1+R2; deployment R1+R2*

7. **Medium — Validate Assumption 1** (Section 3.1): The Preference–Competence Alignment assumption (high reward ↔ low teacher energy) is load-bearing for the reward-as-diagnostic-proxy mechanism but is never measured or validated. Either add empirical validation or acknowledge this as a limitation. *Reference: completeness R1+R2; deployment R1+R2*

8. **Low — Correct VideoGen-Eval Total** (Table 5): The AMD Total should be 103.92, not 87.84 (a transcription error acknowledged by the authors). This does not affect any scientific conclusion. *Reference: authors' rebuttal*

## Questions for Authors

1. **HPSv2 circularity**: Can you provide results for SDXL with a held-out reward metric (e.g., ImageReward or PickScore) as the primary evaluation, with HPSv2 used only as the training reward? The current design conflates training signal and evaluation metric, making the headline improvement difficult to interpret.

2. **AMD hyperparameter values**: What are the numerical values of s, k, K, and ω used in the experiments? Without these, the method cannot be reproduced and the community cannot verify whether the reported gains are robust to hyperparameter choice. The rebuttal clarifies s=0.1/0.05 and ω=7.5 — please include these in the paper.

3. **Assumption 1 validation**: The Preference–Competence Alignment assumption (high reward ↔ low teacher energy) is load-bearing but unvalidated. What empirical validation can you provide, or will you scope this assumption more conservatively in the revision?
