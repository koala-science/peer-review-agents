### Hedges and Uncertainties

1. **Table 1 rounding explanation**: The author rebuttal claims Avg discrepancies arise from computing averages from full-precision values before rounding per-task scores. I cannot verify this without the raw data. If the true averages differ substantially, the relative ordering of methods could change. This is the single highest residual risk.

2. **Cosine alignment empirical coverage**: The adversarial claims empirical support spans "Layer 2, 5, 9, 15, 20, 22 for both Llama3.1-8B-Instruct and Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3 on 2WikiMQA." I did not independently verify these specific layer/dataset combinations from the paper text. The claim-auditor said the evidence was "limited layers/datasets." The adversarial's specific enumeration of layers suggests the paper has more coverage than I credited.

3. **Qwen-Synthetic exact match**: The model-insensitivity explanation is plausible but undemonstrated. Without variance data, we cannot distinguish genuine compression-equivalence from lucky single-run draw.

4. **Ablation table motivation**: The author rebuttal says the ablation was "removed for space." The commented-out LaTeX could also indicate unfavorable results were suppressed. I have no evidence for this interpretation but it cannot be excluded.

5. **Denominator instability**: The assumption-stress-tester identified a concrete numerical instability regime (‖c₁₁‖ − 2‖c₁₂‖ + ‖c₂₂‖ → 0 as α_i → 0) where Eq. 20 is undefined. The paper does not discuss this. Whether this regime actually occurs in practice is unknown.

6. **GQA/MQA transfer**: The paper evaluates on Llama3.1-8B-Instruct which uses GQA. If the spectral theory and gradient-free method require MHA-specific conditions, the claims may not transfer to the very model used for evaluation. This is a potential confound I flagged but did not investigate deeply.

### Disagreement with R3 Panel

The R3 panel on cosine-alignment-validity split 3-1 on whether to rate the paper 2 or 3 for the gradient-free formula. I gave the technical-soundness verdict 3 because:
- Theorem 1 (Hessian) is mathematically sound and uncontested
- The cosine alignment is disclosed, not hidden
- Empirical support is substantial (multiple layers, models, datasets)
- The adversarial's point that the "concrete counterexample" is never actually shown to occur in the merging regime is valid

The proof-checker's verdict 2 would be appropriate if the paper framed Eq. 20 as a proved theorem. Since it explicitly says "our empirical analysis in Fig. 4 reveals," I believe verdict 3 is correct.
