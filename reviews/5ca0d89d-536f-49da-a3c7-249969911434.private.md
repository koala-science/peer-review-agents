# Private Companion: Deep Tabular Research Review

## Uncertainty, Hedges, and Raw Notes

### Confidence Assessment
- **ICML Overall: 3** (Weak Reject) — I am fairly confident (3/5) in this verdict
- The issues I identified are specific and documented, but some (P(π) operationalization, DTR-Bench contamination) are gaps rather than definitive falsifications

### Where I'm Uncertain
1. **Win Rate metric**: I am confident this is mathematically impossible (>1.0 for a proportion), but I'm less certain about the underlying intent. The paper may have defined a non-standard metric where "Win Rate" is not literally a proportion. If so, the definition in the paper is self-contradictory.

2. **ECR = 100.00**: Could be a small-N effect (if only a few visualization instances were evaluated) or a metric computation issue. I flagged it as suspicious but cannot confirm it's an error without N.

3. **DTR-Bench contamination**: I cannot verify this without access to the train/test splits. This is a speculative concern based on the paper's disclosure that DTR-Bench was derived from RealHitBench tables using DeepSeek-3.2.

4. **P(π) operationalization**: The completeness subagent was confident (4/5) that P(π) is undefined. However, it's possible P(π) is intended to be uniform or a simple heuristic that the paper simply didn't spell out. This would be a clarity gap, not a completeness gap.

### What Could Change My Verdict
- If the authors clarify that Win Rate is a non-standard metric (e.g., a normalized score rather than a proportion), the experimental rigor concern diminishes substantially
- If P(π) is actually a simple uniform prior or a basic heuristic, the undefined component concern reduces to a clarity issue
- If DTR-Bench test instances are confirmed disjoint from RealHitBench training data, the contamination concern disappears
- If the theoretical boundedness error is corrected (constant bound instead of growing bound), technical soundness improves to at least 3/4
