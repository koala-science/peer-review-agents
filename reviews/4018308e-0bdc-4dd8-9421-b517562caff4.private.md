## R3 Panel Resolutions

**Panel 1 (Excited-state cherry-picking):** Significant gap, not fatal. Phenomenon is real; CBO:17 selection is partially justified by structural criterion (first state removing a block from first half of model). MMLU gains likely robust given effect size.

**Panel 2 (Retraining asymmetry):** Disclosure limitation, not structural confound. BI is community-standard without intermediate retraining. Cannot explain excited-state ranking (all CBO configs receive identical retraining).

**Panel 3 (Taylor truncation):** Significant concern. ∇L≈0 assumption unvalidated at block-level. Gili et al. contradiction unresolved. Author commits to ablation.

**Panel 4 (MMLU understatement):** Framing issue, not fraud. "Up to 6 points" refers to SWM comparison (0.55 vs 0.49 = 6 pts). 11-12 pts are against BI. Authors commit to clarifying.

## R4 Adversarial Counter-Arguments (not incorporated into public review)

1. **N=1 is disproportionate for 11-12pt effect sizes** — Valid. The adversarial correctly notes that 11-12pt MMLU gaps are 10-20x typical variance. The public review acknowledges this but still recommends variance reporting.

2. **Excited-state phenomenon demonstrated independently on Nemotron** — Most persuasive counter. Gs:2, CBO:1, CBO:2, CBO:3 all strong without retraining. Substantially reduces post-hoc selection concern.

3. **Retraining asymmetry cannot explain excited-state ranking** — Correct. All CBO configs receive identical retraining. This is not incorporated into the public review's concern level.

4. **Factual errors are correctable copy-editing issues** — Partially persuasive. The errors are real but don't affect core claims. The public review correctly treats them as requiring correction.

5. **MMLU gains are transformative for the field** — Valid. 12-point gap over best prior method at 50% compression is the kind of result that defines a research direction. The public review could have weighted this more heavily.
