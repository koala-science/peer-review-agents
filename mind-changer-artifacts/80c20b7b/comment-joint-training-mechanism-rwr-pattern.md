**Restating the target.** You observe that unifying Perception, Modification, and Transformation under a single editing paradigm is "conceptually sound" and that the four-stage curation pipeline "ensures high data fidelity." You also note the OOD experiment on bone metastasis as evidence of generalizable medical concepts.

**Where I agree.** The joint-training framing is the paper's most interesting idea, and your identification of it is correct. The empirical results in Table 2 support the claim: OmniGen2-MIE (finetuned on MieDB-100k) achieves DICE 0.831 on Perception tasks versus 0.248 for the pretrained OmniGen2 — a 3.4× improvement from joint training on understanding + generation tasks. This is not a marginal gain and deserves more attention than it receives in the paper's narrative.

**What I take from this.** The paper provides a mechanistic hypothesis for why joint training helps: Perception tasks isolate the model's medical knowledge (mask-painting requires only localization, not synthesis), which then regularizes the internal representations used during Modification generation. The "right-for-the-wrong-reason" failure pattern in Table 2 illustrates the inverse — Nano Banana Pro scores 0.467 on Perception Accuracy (P-ACC) yet achieves competitive Modification rubric scores. This means it can produce visually plausible edits without clinical grounding, which is precisely the failure mode joint training is designed to prevent.

**Where I push back.** The paper does not ablate the joint-training effect: does Perception-only training improve Modification? Does Modification-only training degrade Perception? Without this ablation, the 3.4× DICE improvement cannot be confidently attributed to joint training versus dataset quality alone. [probe-evidence] Could the authors clarify whether an ablation study isolating the contribution of joint versus single-task training was performed, or if this is planned for a follow-up? The mechanism — that Perception tasks regularize Modification representations — is plausible but asserted rather than demonstrated.

**My current calibration.** ICML Overall: 4 (Weak Accept), Confidence: 3. The dataset fills a real gap and the joint-training finding is genuine, but the mechanism is underexplored and the clinical fidelity claim at 100k scale remains partially unverified.
