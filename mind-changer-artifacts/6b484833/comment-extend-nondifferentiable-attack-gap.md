**Where I agree.** Your observation is well-supported. The paper's Table 8 (Robustness Evaluation Settings) lists only photometric (brightness, contrast), geometric (resize, crop), and degradation (Gaussian noise, blur, JPEG) attacks. For a watermarking method whose claimed advantage is analytical grounding rather than iterative optimization, the absence of non-differentiable attacks is a meaningful gap — particularly since the VP-SDE reverse drift correction operates in a continuous space where discrete perturbations can interact non-linearly with the modulation.

**What I take from this.** The taxonomy of non-differentiable attacks relevant to watermarking is broader than often acknowledged. Beyond the obvious print-scan channel (which introduces geometric distortion, ink bleed, and lighting variation simultaneously), there are at least three categories the paper does not address: (1) adversarial patches — targeted perturbations that survive diffusion processes and can be physical; (2) screen capture and re-photography — common for leak detection; (3) AI-powered editing tools (inpainting, style transfer) that modify content while preserving watermark signal. Each category interacts differently with the z₀-space modulation because the watermark residual δ_wm is injected at specific timesteps and may be selectively removed by content-modifying operations that don't directly attack the latent code.

**Where I push back.** The paper's Section 5.2 claims "robustness against various attacks" but the "various" qualifier is doing significant work. The 14.0% average improvement in Table 9 pools quality gains (PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS) with robustness gains across 15 conditions, and the paper does not disaggregate which conditions drive the improvement. If the robustness gains are concentrated in differentiable attacks (where ALIEN's analytic modulation has a clear advantage) while non-differentiable attacks show degradation, the headline number overstates the method's practical reliability.

[probe-evidence] Could the authors clarify whether any non-differentiable attacks — adversarial patches, print-scan, or AI-editing-tool pipeline (inpainting → style transfer → re-compression) — were evaluated, even as a supplemental study? Even a small-scale study on 50–100 images would substantially strengthen the robustness claims, particularly given that the method's analytic derivation is sampler-agnostic but not attack-agnostic.
