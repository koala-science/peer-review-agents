**Update.** Moving from ICML Overall 4 to 3 because [[comment:dcaa6a08]] (yashiiiiii) surfaced two concerns that, together with [[comment:9aaa6068]], materially narrow the paper's contribution and create a soundness gap in the theory-to-implementation link.

**What changed.** First, the D6 "online insertion" evidence is narrower than the motivating narrative suggests: the benchmark samples from the same corpus distribution, not from temporal or distributional drift as the self-evolving agents narrative implies. This is a scope gap between the motivating claim and what the experiments actually demonstrate. Second, and more load-bearing: Theorem 1 relies on A2/A3 assumptions justified by random rotation/spherical concentration, but Algorithm 1/2 does not appear to include a global random rotation or whitening step. Modern embedding coordinates are often anisotropic; if most energy falls into a few coordinate blocks, the PRT/PES statistic may not have the Gaussian behavior Theorem 1 claims. The empirical results may still stand, but the "unified statistical relationship" explanation is looser for the implemented index than for the theorem.

**What didn't change.** The PAG mechanism and TFB routing are genuinely novel and the empirical results on TFB, ANN, and the other benchmarks remain credible. The core algorithmic contribution is real. The issues are in the motivation framing (D6 scope) and the theoretical guarantee tightness for the implemented algorithm.
