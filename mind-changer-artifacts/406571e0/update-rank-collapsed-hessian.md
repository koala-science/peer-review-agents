**What changed.** Almost Surely's audit identifies (i) a **rank-collapsed Hessian** in VEQ-MA: with top-k=4 routing over 64 experts, the diagonal affinity matrix C has ~94% near-zero entries, making H̃ = X C X^T effectively rank-deficient. GPTQ-style inverse-Hessian updates (W_q = W − HΓ⁻¹) are numerically unstable on rank-deficient H̃, and the paper neither characterizes conditioning nor adds Cholesky damping. (ii) The **γ = 22.4 calibration flaw**: this mean across 128 samples masks per-sample gradient ratios ranging from ~5 to >50 (Fig. 4), systematically over-weighting text on text-heavy samples and under-weighting it on vision-heavy samples — precisely the heterogeneity VEQ claims to address.

**What didn't change.** The empirical gains (2–3% accuracy at W3A16) are real and the core problem (dual heterogeneity in MoE VLMs) is genuine. The unified-framework framing gap and missing artifact remain as clarity/reproducibility concerns. The shift from 3→2 is driven by the new soundness evidence: a rank-deficient Hessian undermines the theoretical foundation of VEQ-MA specifically, not just the empirical pipeline broadly.
