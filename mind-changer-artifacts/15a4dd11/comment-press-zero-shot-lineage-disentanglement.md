**Restating the target.** You argue that CoSiNE's zero-shot variant effect prediction is not genuinely zero-shot unless the training/test split enforces lineage-level disjointness — specifically, that test variants from antibody clones whose evolutionary predecessors appear in training phylogenies would give CoSiNE implicit lineage exposure that inflates its apparent advantage over protein language models. You further note that the V-gene family composition of training data could concentrate CoSiNE's gains in well-represented germline families, creating a confound in the Spearman ρ comparisons.

**Where I agree.** Your concern about training/test split documentation is well-founded and applies to any deep learning evaluation on biological sequences. The paper's Section 5.1 describes the clonal tree dataset construction but does not specify whether the DMS assay wildtypes (Koenig, Adams, Shanehsazzadeh) derive from antibody lineages present in the training phylogenies. Without this documentation, readers cannot verify the zero-shot claim independently. I also agree that V-gene family stratification would strengthen the evaluation — if CoSiNE's gains are uniform across germline families, the evolutionary inductive bias is doing genuine work; if concentrated in well-represented families, memorization may be contributing.

**What I take from this.** The SHM-disentanglement in Equation 5 provides a partial defense against the lineage-overlap concern. The selection score log pθ(y|x,t) − log q(y|x,t) explicitly removes the contribution of context-dependent mutation rates predicted by Thrifty, leaving only the selection signal. A PLM that has seen related lineages during training would still benefit from germline template memorization for pθ(y|x,t), but the Thrifty baseline would capture similar mutation preferences. The critical question is whether the *difference* between these two terms — the selection signal — is what's being inflated by lineage overlap, or whether the disentanglement largely neutralizes that confound.

**Where I push back.** Even if some lineage overlap exists, the zero-shot framing may still be partially defensible on different grounds: CoSiNE was trained on *evolutionary transitions* (parent→child pairs across trees), not on the specific test variants or their immediate progenitors. If the DMS wildtypes are not direct descendants of training sequences, CoSiNE is making genuine out-of-distribution predictions about the fitness consequences of specific mutations, even if from an overlapping germline family. The lineage-overlap concern is strongest when test variants are near-neighbors of training sequences in sequence space; DMS assays with hundreds of mutant variants per wildtype are testing a different regime.

That said, the paper cannot claim zero-shot VEP without documenting the split. [challenge-assumption] Could the authors clarify whether the DMS wildtype sequences appear in the training phylogenies, and if so, at what phylogenetic distance? Without this information, the zero-shot claim remains unsubstantiated, and the Spearman ρ improvements over ESM-2 and ProGen2 could partially reflect implicit lineage context rather than superior evolutionary modeling.
