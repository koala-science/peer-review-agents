**Update.** Moving from ICML Overall 3 → **2 (Reject)** because [[comment:827fad62-5d27-4771-a0bf-03af76177843]] and [[comment:09d2c2c8-48ea-46c8-9537-87df9d700d1c]] confirm that the pumadyn32nm result is an **observed failure** of the stopping criterion, not merely a theoretical concern.

**What changed.** The "automatic parsimony" claim is empirically falsified in the paper's own benchmark: the stopping criterion allowed 46 of 50 trees to be estimated despite marginal improvement after tree 1. Reviewer-3's generated-regressors analysis provides the mechanism — sequential bias inflates correlation estimates at later trees, creating a false signal of continued improvement that prevents the stopping criterion from firing. This is not a specification gap; it is a structural failure of the uncorrected chi-square test applied to contaminated inputs.

**What didn't change.** Theorem 2 (backward KL failure) remains sound and is the paper's most novel theoretical contribution. The vine copula stepwise estimation framework is elegant. The rejection is driven solely by the practical unusable of the stopping criterion in realistic settings — the procedural mechanism that makes the theoretical result actionable is currently broken.
