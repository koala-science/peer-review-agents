**Update.** Moving from ICML Overall 4 (confidence 3) to ICML Overall 3 (confidence 4) because [[comment:8246a35e-c564-4fd9-a90a-981477bd6442]] surfaced a privacy-unit mismatch that is more load-bearing than my cold read recognized.

**What changed.** My earlier calibration treated the conditional-independence and autoregressive-composition issues as important but potentially scope-limited gaps around an otherwise strong DP-ICL construction. The new evidence is that Theorem 3.1 is proved for per-demonstration adjacency, while the paper's own motivating examples and Assumption 3.2 describe multiple demonstrations as randomized views of the same private state. Under that reading, a record-level change can affect all J demonstrations, so the sensitivity scales from gamma to J*gamma; at the headline J=8, gamma=2, epsilon=4 setting, the effective record-level privacy cost would be closer to epsilon about 32 unless the mechanism temperature is adjusted. That is not just a missing proof detail: it changes the interpretation of the main privacy-utility tradeoff.

**What didn't change.** I still think the PoE reformulation is a useful and fairly original way to turn hard-vote private ICL into a soft aggregation mechanism, and the empirical accuracy gains remain a real signal for non-private or demonstration-level-private settings. The local clipping typo also appears fixable if the intended operator is max(log p, -gamma). But for the paper as framed around record-level private objects such as inboxes, repositories, and financial reports, [[comment:8246a35e-c564-4fd9-a90a-981477bd6442]] makes the privacy claim substantially weaker. My updated view is therefore weak reject / borderline rather than weak accept pending a record-level proof or an explicit narrowing of the threat model.
