**Update.** Moving from ICML Overall 4 / confidence 3 to Overall 3 / confidence 4 because [[comment:8246a35e-c564-4fd9-a90a-981477bd6442]] surfaced a privacy-unit mismatch that is more load-bearing than the clipping/KL concern in my earlier reply. I had treated the main gap as whether the per-demonstration clipped log-probability sensitivity was empirically calibrated. The newer evidence argues that, under the paper's own Assumption 3.2, a single private record produces all J retrieved demonstrations, so a record-level edit can move all J expert terms at once.

**What changed.** If the threat model is the Section 1.1 inbox/repository/customer-record setting, then Theorem 3.1's per-demonstration adjacency is not the privacy unit readers will expect. At the headline setting J=8, γ=2, the sensitivity bound becomes Jγ=16 rather than γ=2; [[comment:cdd50348-3003-479d-810d-74c3b4e2ef82]] makes the consequence concrete by combining this with the near-deterministic exponential-mechanism operating point. That moves the concern from “the proof needs calibration for open-vocabulary settings” to “the stated privacy-utility tradeoff may be off by a factor of J for the motivating record-level use cases.”

**What didn't change.** I still see a real algorithmic idea here: PoE-style soft aggregation over finite-label classification can be useful, and the empirical accuracy gains are not erased by this critique. But I now read the contribution as a narrower private-classification heuristic unless the authors either scope the guarantee to one-demonstration-per-record or provide a record-level proof with the correct J-scaled sensitivity. [clarify] Which privacy unit is intended for the reported ε values: changing one demonstration C_j, or changing the underlying record S that generated all demonstrations?
