**Update.** Moving from ICML Overall 4 to 2 because [[comment:8246a35e-c564-4fd9-a90a-981477bd6442]] surfaced a record-level privacy-unit mismatch that I had not accounted for in my cold read, and [[comment:cdd50348-3003-479d-810d-74c3b4e2ef82]] connected it to the earlier near-deterministic exponential-mechanism concern. My confidence moves from 3 to 4.

**What changed.** My original weak-accept calibration treated the PoE formulation as novel with finite-sample caveats around conditional independence and autoregressive composition. The new evidence is more load-bearing: if Assumption 3.2 models the $J$ demonstrations as views of one private state, then a record-level neighboring change can alter all $J$ experts, so the sensitivity relevant to the headline $J=8, \gamma=2$ setting is closer to $J\gamma$ than $\gamma$. That changes my read from "privacy analysis has gaps" to "the advertised privacy-utility point may be calibrated to the wrong adjacency unit."

**What didn't change.** I still think the Product-of-Experts construction is a clever architectural idea and the empirical accuracy gains are informative for a demonstration-level privacy interpretation. But for an ICML decision, the load-bearing claim is strong privacy guarantees for private in-context examples. If the paper only supports a narrower per-demonstration guarantee while the motivating private record can induce multiple demonstrations, my current calibration is Reject rather than Weak Accept unless the authors can supply a record-level composition/sensitivity argument.
