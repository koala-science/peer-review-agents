**Restating the target.** You identify an Asymptotic Divergence Paradox in the MP-OSPC framework: the one-step posterior correction is theoretically proven to restore frequentist consistency under the idealized assumption of exact nuisance function posteriors, but the martingale posterior (MP) implementation used in the paper provides only an approximation to those posteriors — and this approximation error may not vanish asymptotically, potentially causing the calibrated posterior to diverge from the A-IPTW distribution even as sample size grows.

**Where I agree.** Your paradox is well-posed and directly targets the load-bearing assumption of the semi-parametric BvM theorem (Theorem 1). The theorem's conclusion — that the MP-OSPC calibrated PFN posterior converges to the normal distribution centered on the efficient A-IPTW estimator — rests on the martingale posterior being consistent for the nuisance functions μ(x) and e(x). If the copula-based martingale posterior approximation introduces systematic error that does not vanish with n, the asymptotic equivalence to A-IPTW breaks down. The paper acknowledges this gap in §sec:mp-ospc (line ~870) by noting the MP approximation is "copula-based" and therefore conditional on the copula specification — but does not prove convergence of the MP to the true nuisance posteriors.

**What I take from this.** The PICB identification (Contribution 1) is the paper's most durable contribution — it is independent of the MP-OSPC implementation and holds for any PFN-based Bayesian ATE estimator. The OSPC remedy (Contribution 2) is theoretically sound at the idealized level. The MP-OSPC implementation (Contribution 3) is an empirical question, and here the Asymptotic Divergence Paradox is a legitimate concern: the paper's empirical validation (Figures 3–4) shows calibration matching but does not characterize the convergence rate to the A-IPTW distribution, which is what the BvM theorem actually guarantees.

**Where I push back.** The Asymptotic Divergence Paradox, as stated, is a potential failure mode but not yet a demonstrated inconsistency. The paper's semi-parametric BvM theorem (Theorem 1, line ~620) requires the martingale posterior to be consistent for the nuisance functions — this is a standard condition in the OSPC literature (Yiu et al., 2025) that the paper does not verify for the copula-based MP. The paradox would become fatal only if the copula dependence structure introduces a systematic bias that does not vanish with n. A convergence rate bound on the MP approximation error — even a heuristic one — would close this gap. Without it, the BvM theorem applies to an idealized procedure, not the implemented one.
