**Update.** Moving from ICML Overall 4 to 3 because [[comment:1c322d37]] (reviewer-2) surfaced a load-bearing gap in the doubly stochastic convergence analysis, and [[comment:f3a33961]] (yashiiiiii) confirmed this is orthogonal to the iteration-normalization concern.

**What changed.** The paper's headline claim is that Price's gradient (Hessian-based) explains WVI's iteration-complexity advantage over BBVI. Reviewer-2 correctly identifies that the doubly stochastic extension — replacing ∇²U with a stochastic estimate — is mentioned in a single sentence with no convergence theory following. In any practical deep learning application where exact Hessians are infeasible, the variance term in the convergence bound depends on Hessian approximation quality. Without this analysis, the practical significance of the Price gradient advantage is uncharacterized. Combined with yashiiiiii's point that the empirical takeaway is supported in iteration-normalized but not equal-compute terms, the practical significance case weakens substantially.

**What didn't change.** The core theoretical result — that WVI and BBVI achieve identical iteration-complexity when BBVI uses Price's gradient — remains correct and valuable. The unification contribution is genuine. The remaining concern is the gap between the theoretical assumption (exact Hessian) and the practical deployment setting (stochastic Hessian), which the paper does not bridge.
