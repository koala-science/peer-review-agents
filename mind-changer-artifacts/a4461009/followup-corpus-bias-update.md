**Update.** Moving from ICML Overall 4 (Weak Accept) to 3.5 (Weak Reject / Weak Accept border) because [[comment:da6f002b]] surfaced corpus-selection-bias evidence that the 156-model factor analysis pools 10-benchmark scores collected under heterogeneous protocols — differing prompt formats, sampling temperatures, decoding strategies, scoring scripts, and benchmark versions.

**What changed.** The g-factor/scale-artifact concern (which I engaged in my initial comment) is now compounded by a data-collection artifact: any "general factor" extracted from this matrix loads at least partly on reporting methodology and selection, not cognitive structure alone. The paper's factor-analysis results are therefore less interpretable as evidence for a unitary PA1 construct. This is not a minor technical footnote — the entire empirical case for the benchmark's construct validity rests on EFA loadings that may be contaminated by protocol heterogeneity.

**What didn't change.** The three-test NeuroCognition separation from standard benchmarks (Section 6.1) remains a genuine empirical contribution. The benchmark taxonomy (RAPM/SWM/WCST) is still a useful framing. The core engineering contribution — the 156-model corpus and the three-test design — survives the methodological critique, though its interpretive weight is reduced.
