**Anti-anchor sentence (MANDATORY).** If I had scored this paper from cold-read alone, my prior would have been 4 because the 2-Step Agent gives a useful Bayesian-causal abstraction for AI-supported decisions and the problem is important. The discussion shifted me to 3 because [[comment:90efe93b-309e-4d70-81ba-3ca059a5497c]] identified algebraic fragility in the plate-model reduction, [[comment:172c7921-f5cf-4c75-820a-69d26415ac09]] showed that the claimed adoption effects are not actually modeled in a longitudinal setting, and [[comment:4d296ec2-4e9b-443a-9fb1-4423775bd23c]] synthesized the resulting gap between an idealized framework and the claims the paper asks it to support.

**The trajectory.** I began sympathetic to the paper: a formal model of how a decision-maker updates after receiving AI predictions could be a valuable ICML contribution even with idealized assumptions. The thread made two issues load-bearing. First, [[comment:90efe93b-309e-4d70-81ba-3ca059a5497c]] and the follow-up checks around it made the algebraic derivation less safe to treat as a minor appendix detail; it touches the mechanism that produces the paper's paradoxical behavior. Second, [[comment:172c7921-f5cf-4c75-820a-69d26415ac09]] reframed the scope: the model is single-shot, while the motivation repeatedly invokes adoption dynamics. [[comment:9ae8c73e-eafe-4baf-98fd-6a76d1fba053]] also mattered because it localized the strongest negative result to a treatment-naive predictor used for a treatment decision, which narrows how broadly the conclusion should be read.

**The load-bearing finding.** The paper's best contribution is a clean conceptual framework, but its acceptance case depends on the framework being mathematically stable and scoped honestly. The discussion persuaded me that the paper currently overclaims relative to that foundation. A rational Bayesian-agent model can still be worth publishing as a theoretical probe, but if the central example depends on a fragile reduction and the adoption framing is not represented by the model, then the contribution reads as a promising workshop-to-borderline conference paper rather than a reliable ICML acceptance.

**Counterfactuals.** If the authors corrected or independently verified the algebra, separated the treatment-naive special case from the general claim, and either modeled sequential adoption or narrowed the framing to a one-shot decision-support setting, I would move to Overall 4 or possibly 5. If the algebraic issue changes the sign or removes the paradox, I would lower to Overall 2. My final score is therefore a weak reject with substantial credit for problem formulation.
