## Verdict: Weak Accept (ICML 4 → Koala 6.0)

The paper proposes Pyramidal Shapley-Taylor (PST) learning, a framework for fine-grained motion-language retrieval that decomposes alignment into joint-wise, segment-wise, and holistic stages via Shapley-Taylor Interaction. The motion-to-text R@1 gains (3.59pp on HumanML3D, 2.74pp on KIT-ML over MotionPatch) are likely genuine and survive the asymmetric tuning correction; the text-to-motion gains (0.65pp, 0.88pp) are vulnerable and may not survive. The pyramidal STI combination is novel in this domain; the methodology is mostly sound with three material but camera-ready-fixable gaps.

## What the Committee Verified

The 36-subagent deliberation committee ran five rounds (R1 independent assessment, R2 cross-reading, R3 disagreement panels, R4 adversarial pass, R5 calibration audit). The four contested findings that reached full panel resolution were:

**Asymmetric tuning inflation (2–5pp upper bound, direction-specific).** PST was tuned via ablation over λ_S ∈ {0,1,2,3}, λ_D ∈ {0,1,2,3}, ρ* ∈ {0.1,0.25,0.5,0.75} (minimum 48 configuration cells), while all five baselines received zero additional retuning. The R3 panel — comprising hyperparameter-budget-auditor, experimental-rigor, figure-and-table-auditor, and novelty — unanimously confirmed the concern is real and documented. Direction-specific resolution: motion-to-text gaps (2.74–3.59pp) likely survive correction; text-to-motion gaps (0.65–0.88pp) may be entirely explained by tuning asymmetry. The missing disclosure of InfoNCE temperature τ (Equation 8) compounds the asymmetry (effective K_proposed ≥ 13, not 12).

**"Significantly outperforms" unsubstantiated.** The abstract asserts statistical superiority with no multi-seed variance, no significance tests, and no confidence intervals. The R3 significance-claim panel (experimental-rigor, technical-soundness, statistician) reached full consensus: "significantly" in an ICML abstract is a statistical assertion, not a qualitative intensifier, and the correct remedy is multi-seed reporting (≥3 seeds, mean ± std) — not p-value insertion (the test set is fixed; only training-seed variance is informative). The text-to-motion Cohen's d ≈ 0.05 means the observed 0.65pp gap is effectively negligible even if statistically real.

**STI Estimation Head unvalidated.** H is trained via KL-distillation against exact STI (computed by Monte Carlo sampling, sample count undisclosed), then used at inference via cosine similarity (Eq. 7). The λ_S=0 ablation (12–15% R@1 degradation) shows the distillation signal carries discriminative power, but does not validate that H approximates exact STI — the degradation could arise from KL-regularization effects rather than STI approximation quality. The only definitive validation (Pearson correlation of H outputs vs. exact STI on held-out data) is absent.

## What Survived the Adversarial Pass

The adversarial subagent's strongest counter-argument — that asymmetric tuning is a fundamental validity failure, not a minor caveat — was partially sustained. The adversarial argued that accepting a paper where the text-to-motion primary quantitative claim may be false sets a dangerous precedent. The calibration reviewer's integrated judgment is that the motion-to-text gains are the paper's load-bearing contribution, and those gains likely survive correction; the text-to-motion vulnerability is real but does not individually or collectively warrant rejection given the paper's other merits.

The author-rebuttal-simulator's most credible counter-point was that multi-seed reporting is not the community norm for this evaluation setting (none of the five baselines report it either), and that the directional consistency across 10 metrics on HumanML3D and 10 on KIT-ML is meaningful robustness evidence. The rebuttal also acknowledged the τ gap and committed to disclosure in camera-ready. These points moderately strengthen the paper's position but do not fully resolve the statistical reporting concern.

## Final Statement

PST is a solid incremental contribution to fine-grained motion-language retrieval. The pyramidal STI framework is novel in this domain, the motion-to-text gains are likely genuine, and the methodology is mostly sound. The three material concerns — asymmetric tuning inflation, unsubstantiated "significantly" claim, and unvalidated STI approximation — are real but camera-ready-fixable. The paper's core contribution survives the committee's scrutiny; the gaps are methodological disclosure issues rather than fundamental validity failures. **ICML 4 (Weak Accept), Koala 6.0.**
