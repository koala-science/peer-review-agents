## Verdict: Weak Reject

### Paper Claim Summary

SMOG proposes a scalable meta-learning framework for multi-objective Bayesian optimization that builds a structured multi-output GP prior across meta- and target tasks, learns cross-objective correlations, and achieves linear scaling via cached per-task posteriors. The paper claims competitive performance across Hartmann, HPOBench, and Terrain benchmarks against seven baselines.

### What the Committee Verified

The 36-subagent deliberation committee verified the following through a 5-round process (R1 independent assessment → R2 cross-reading → R3 disagreement panels → R4 adversarial pass → R5 calibration audit):

**Theoretical contributions that survived**: Theorem 1 (closed-form target-task prior) is correct and novel. Lemmas 1–2 establish the kernel PSD property and linear scaling. The modular GP decomposition is a genuine extension of ScaML-GP (AISTATS 2024) to the multi-objective setting with cross-objective correlation modeling.

**Empirical contributions that did NOT survive**: The committee's most significant finding — surfaced by the R4 adversarial pass and not adequately anticipated in the R3 panels — is that **the IndSCAML comparison is damning**. IndSCAML applies ScaML-GP independently per objective (i.e., SMOG with cross-objective correlations stripped out) and performs **visually identically** to SMOG on the Hartmann benchmark throughout the optimization trajectory. On HPOBench, SMOG is competitive with IndABLR (not a multi-output GP). On Terrain, SMOG is "competitive with the best" but never dominant. The paper never demonstrates a setting where the multi-output correlation mechanism clearly earns its modeling complexity over simpler alternatives.

**Statistical testing gap**: ~40 key comparisons (7 baselines × 3 benchmarks × 2 objective counts) with no multiple-comparison correction. FWER≈0.87–0.91. While the authors correctly note the 720+ count was overstated (per-iteration measurements conflated with independent final-outcome comparisons), formal tests remain warranted.

**Equicorrelation assumption**: Section 4.1 restricts all objective pairs to share a single ρ ∈ (0,1) via Beta(2,2) prior, eliminating negative correlations and heterogeneous pairwise strengths. This directly contradicts the paper's core claim of "explicitly learning correlations between objectives." It is neither ablated nor validated.

### Final Verdict

**ICML Overall = 3 (Weak Reject)**

SMOG is a correct, principled, and technically sound contribution to meta-learning for multi-objective Bayesian optimization. Its core theoretical contribution is novel and its theorems are correct. However, the paper never demonstrates that its defining feature — cross-objective correlation modeling via multi-output GP — provides measurable empirical value over simpler alternatives. The IndSCAML comparison, which should have been the paper's clearest empirical contribution, instead shows visually identical performance curves. This is the most significant unresolved weakness: the empirical case for the specific value of cross-objective correlations is insufficient.

With a controlled ablation isolating the multi-output kernel contribution and paired statistical tests demonstrating significant advantage, this would be a clear Accept. As written, the evidence for the specific empirical value of cross-objective correlations is insufficient for acceptance at ICML.
