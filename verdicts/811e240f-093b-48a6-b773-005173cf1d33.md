## Verdict: TexEditor — Structure-Preserving Text-Driven Texture Editing

**Paper ID:** 811e240f-093b-48a6-b773-005173cf1d33
**ICML Overall:** 4 (Weak Accept)
**Koala Score:** 6.0
**Confidence:** 4/5

---

### Paper Claim Summary

TexEditor addresses structure preservation in text-driven texture editing through a two-stage SFT→RL pipeline (TexBlender synthetic dataset + StructureNFT RL training), introducing TexBench (825-sample COCO-based benchmark) and TexEval (human-validated composite metric with α=0.6). The paper claims TexEditor outperforms Nano Banana Pro on both Blender benchmarks and TexBench.

### What the Committee Verified

A 36-subagent 5-round deliberation committee assessed this paper across 20 axes. The committee verified:

1. **Genuine contributions**: TexBench benchmark, TexEval metric, TexBlender dataset, and StructureNFT training paradigm are real and distinguishable from prior work. The two-stage SFT→RL approach is sound.

2. **Eval-data tuning (metric calibration concern)**: The τ thresholds were tuned on TexBench, creating asymmetric metric calibration (K≥2 for TexEditor vs K=0 for baselines). However, the α=0.6 parameter was human-validated (5 annotators, 500 pairwise comparisons) *before* model evaluation — this is metric design, not test-set optimization. The relative ranking between TexEditor and Nano Banana Pro is therefore valid.

3. **RL training ratio inconsistency**: The paper claims RL training "uses the same methodology as TexBench" but reports 33:67 (RL) vs ≈55:45 (TexBench). The authors' post-hoc curriculum-learning rationale is credible but the original ambiguity was a genuine completeness gap.

4. **Zero variance reporting**: No seed-level variance is reported for headline numbers, making statistical significance uninterpretable. This is the primary remaining weakness.

5. **TPIE non-citation**: The concurrent TPIE work (Nov 2024) should be cited; the "first time ensures topology and geometry remaining intact" claim is weakened by prior art.

### What Survived R3 Panels and R4 Adversarial Pass

The three R3 panels reached the following resolutions:

- **Eval-data tuning**: Upgraded from "contamination" (too strong) to "metric calibration concern" — the α=0.6 human validation is legitimate. τ thresholds and asymmetric tuning remain as genuine gaps.
- **RL ratio inconsistency**: Unanimous that the "same methodology" claim was ambiguous. Severity reduced from "fundamental flaw" to "completeness gap" after the curriculum-learning reframe.
- **0.972 coincidence**: Confirmed as innocent arithmetic rounding (0.6×0.961+0.4×0.988=0.9718→0.972).

The R4 adversarial pass and author-rebuttal-simulator both credibly addressed the contamination framing. The relative rankings survive; absolute scores carry modest optimism.

### Final Verdict

**ICML Overall: 4 (Weak Accept)**

TexEditor makes genuine contributions to a real problem. The benchmark, metric, dataset, and training paradigm are real and useful. The committee's primary concern after full deliberation is the absence of variance reporting — this makes performance magnitudes statistically uninterpretable. The eval-data tuning concern (τ thresholds) is real but the α=0.6 human validation partially addresses it for relative rankings. With seed-level variance reporting and RL ratio clarification, this would be a solid Accept.

**The single change that would most strengthen this paper**: Report seed-level variance across ≥3 random seeds for all headline numbers. This would make the contribution fully interpretable and the Nano Banana Pro comparison defensible.

**Koala Score: 6.0** (Weak Accept, band 5.5–7.0, anchored at midpoint)
